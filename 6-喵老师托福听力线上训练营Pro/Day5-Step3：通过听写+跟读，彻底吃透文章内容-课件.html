Step 3：通过听写+跟读，彻底吃透文章内容

听写练习


听写的目的是找到我们的听力盲区，让我们在做跟读的时候能够更有重点。听写时，每个句子播放一般不要超过三遍（特别长的句子除外），如果听完后依然写不全，可以换行写下一句。


听写完成后比对文本，标清楚没听到、没听懂的地方，以及没听懂的原因（例如单词不认识、单词认识但音不熟悉、语音现象造成的听力困难）。


如果时间实在特别紧张，无法完成听写，那么请务必完成下面的跟读练习。


跟读练习


Conversation虽然短，但语速却比Lecture要快，很多高分段的同学最终Lecture能做全对，却往往在conversation上栽跟头。在这部分，我带着大家逐句分析所有造成听力障碍的语音现象。强烈推荐所有同学都能一起张嘴跟着练习。只有熟练掌握美式英语的发音规则、语调上的重点，才能让我们在听的过程中更加有的放矢，更轻松。


逐句跟读音频+文本

（在这部分，我尽可能保留了原文完整的句子/意群，所以有些音频会比较长，也是为了让大家练习听一个完整意群的能力。如果有困难可以自己在中间断一下。）


1. Listen to a conversation between a student and his creative writing professor.

2.  Hi, Kane. We missed you on Thursday.

3. Sorry I couldn’t make the class, Professor Bennett. But I was out of town. Did I miss a lot?

4. You’ll be fine. But I paired students so as to co-write an epistolary fiction.

5. You’ll be working with Brian Simpson, since he was also absent. So get in touch with him soon, OK?

6.  Because between the two of you, you’ll be writing about pages total, a story. Um, you’re quick (更可能是acquainted，熟悉的) with epistolary literary form, right?

7. Yeah. An epistolary piece is made up of letters.

8.   I’m... last year I wrote a paper on democracy in the United States and I used a book called Letters of Henry Adams.

9.  He was a journalist and historian who lived in late 1800s and early 1900s, and some of his letters in that book discussed his democratic ideals.

10. Letters of Henry Adams. But remember that book is nonfiction. Those were actual letters written by only one writer, Henry Adams.

11.  Your assignment for my class is to write a story through exchange of letters between two fictional characters. 

12.  So first you and Brian will have to decide what relationship your characters will have, best friends, brothers, whatever, and then develop a story.13. Now your classmates, Debbie and Maria. Debbie’s gonna play the role of a rock and roll singer and Maria her agent, um back in the 1950s.

14. Those characters could be a good choice for them since Debbie and Maria are both big fans of early rock and roll and know a lot about it.

15. Oh, I see. Okay. What about coworkers? Like my character can work part-time in the student employment office, like I do, and maybe he could exchange emails with his officemate or something, like Kane could email to Brian.

16.  ‘Could I borrow your pencil’ and they’re sitting in desks right next to each other. That could be funny.

17. P: Okay. But you know I’ve recently read an epistolary novel consisting only of emails. It’s called Fast Office. S: Fast Office.

18. Yeah. It’s set in an accounting firm and it’s filled with office politics, comments about technology.

19.  Anyway the concept of Fast Office actually had potential, but ultimately it was more like an endless stream of disjointed remarks. I mean talk about its thin plot. Huh.

20. But that doesn’t mean you and Brian couldn’t make an email exchange work. 21.  There is another epistolary novel called Life on the Refrigerator Door and it comprises just notes that a mother and daughter leave for each other, attached to their refrigerator door.

22.   And what (one?) isn’t expecting much but through these short notes, the characters manage to tell us a good story and convey their love and concern for each other in a really powerful way.
