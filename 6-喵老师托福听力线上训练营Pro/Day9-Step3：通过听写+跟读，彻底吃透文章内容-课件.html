Step 3：通过听写+跟读，彻底吃透文章内容

听写练习

听写的目的是找到我们的听力盲区，让我们在做跟读的时候能够更有重点。听写时，每个句子播放一般不要超过三遍（特别长的句子除外），如果听完后依然写不全，可以换行写下一句。


听写完成后比对文本，标清楚没听到、没听懂的地方，以及没听懂的原因（例如单词不认识、单词认识但音不熟悉、语音现象造成的听力困难）。


如果时间实在特别紧张，无法完成听写，那么请务必完成下面的跟读练习。


跟读练习


在这部分，我带着大家逐句分析所有造成听力障碍的语音现象。强烈推荐所有同学都能一起张嘴跟着练习。只有熟练掌握美式英语的发音规则、语调上的重点，才能让我们在听的过程中更加有的放矢，更轻松。

逐句跟读音频+文本

（在这部分，我尽可能保留了原文完整的句子/意群，所以有些音频会比较长，也是为了让大家练习听一个完整意群的能力。如果有困难可以自己在中间断一下。）

1. Listen to a conversation between a student and his ancient history professor. 

2. I know you put a lot of effort into making all the arrangements for the play in New York, getting us the special group discount plus getting the department to help with the price of the tickets. 

3. Well, it's not every day that we have the opportunity to attend a production of Aeschylus's the Persians performed by the National Theater of Greece, no less. 

4. You know, it's the earliest surviving Greek tragedy, the very first one. 

5. Oh, I didn't realize that the Persians was the oldest of all the Greek plays. That's kind of neat. Aeschylus lived in like the fifth century B.C.E, right? 

6. Yes. He lived during the series of wars that the Persians chronicles, the Persian wars. 

7. And that's what makes this play unusual. It talks about historical events that were occurring more or less around the time it was written. 

8. We'll talk about it next week's class. 

9. Oh, that's interesting, is it the only Greek play that talks about, like current events at that time? 

10. That's right. Well, the only play that we know of anyway, if other playwrights wrote on similar topics, those plays have been lost. 

11. But we're getting sidetracked. You wanted to tell me something?

12. Yes. I know you're not going to be too happy about this, but... I just don't think I would enjoy this play. 

13. I'm sorry to hear that, um, what's the problem? 

14. Well, seeing a play in a language I don't understand... I just don't think I'd get very much out of it, so I... 

15. Well, you'll surely understand some things. We'll be studying the play beforehand, so you'll be familiar with it. 

16. Anyway, attendance is required. It's in the syllabus. And there's a short paper on it due, too. That's also mentioned in the syllabus.

17. I sort of misplaced my syllabus. 

18. Well, you'll need to get a copy from a classmate. Anyway the play will have supertitles, that's common practice nowadays. 

19. - Supertitles? - Like subtitles in movies, but they usually project them across the top of the stage. 

20. Oh yeah, I know what they are. They had them when our Intro to Music class went to the opera in New York. 

21. Well, there you go. See, people go to the opera all the time, and they don't necessarily know the language in which the opera is being performed. 

22. - What language was your opera in? - Russian, another language I don't understand. 

23. - And, did you enjoy it? - Well, it was a little frustrating at first, not understanding the words, but once I got past that, yeah, I actually did get a lot out of it.
