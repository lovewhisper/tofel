Step 3：通过听写+跟读，彻底吃透文章内容

听写练习


听写的目的是找到我们的听力盲区，让我们在做跟读的时候能够更有重点。听写时，每个句子播放一般不要超过三遍（特别长的句子除外），如果听完后依然写不全，可以换行写下一句。听写的过程中，注意思考句间关系，并试着预测下一句的内容。


听写完成后比对文本，标清楚没听到、没听懂的地方，以及没听懂的原因（例如单词不认识、单词认识但音不熟悉、语音现象造成的听力困难）。


跟读练习


如果实在没有时间做听写，也请务必完成跟读。标注版文本如，橙色为考点句，红色为信号词以及非考点但比较重要的句子。


逐句跟读音频+文本

（在这部分，我尽可能保留了原文完整的句子/意群，所以有些音频会比较长，也是为了让大家练习听一个完整意群的能力。如果有困难可以自己在中间断一下。）


1. Listen to part of a lecture in a United States History class. 

2. Okay, you want to talk about a really significant invention? How about mechanical refrigeration and air conditioning?

3. Think about it, our country would be a very different place without it.

4. For example, how about the fact that hotter regions of the United States, like Florida, would only have a fraction of their current populations…

5. Or that we probably wouldn't have many skyscrapers since you can't open the windows 50 stories up because of high winds, and an open window was about the only way to cool a room in the past.

6. And industrial applications of refrigerated air have been extremely important.

7. Let's look at the printing industry for example. Paper expands and contracts according to the amount of moisture in the air.

8. So before air conditioning, it could be impossible to align the inks for printing in color.

9. So even something as mundane as color magazines, something we take for granted, it's the regulation of temperature and humidity in the print shop that makes them possible.

10. Now it turns out making something cold is not so easy. I mean, to make something hotter, you can heat it with fire for example, but to cool something?

11. Sure if it's winter you can get some ice and you're okay, but how practical is that? And what if it isn't winter?

12. Now, one of the guys who mulled over this problem, was a Doctor John Gorrie.

13. Dr. John Gorrie moved to Apalachicola, Florida in the 1830's.

14. In those days, Florida with its hot, humid summers, its snakes, alligators and mosquitos, and its tropical diseases, was a hard place to live.

15. But Apalachicola was actually the second largest port in the Gulf of Mexico.

16.  Much of the cotton grown in the Southern U.S. States, and that was a lot, was shipped out of Apalachicola.

17. Now in the 1840's, Dr. Gorrie, in deciding how to treat those tropical diseases, deduced that since they occurred in the summer, they must be in some way caused by the heat.

18. The cure therefore, would be to in effect change the season. Take summer and the heat of it away.

19.  Oh we are lucky he drew that conclusion, instead of the correct one, which is probably why he didn't go down in history for any great medical breakthroughs.

20. Anyway, his first experiments with these treatments led him to develop cold rooms, or rooms cooled with ice. In some cases, the ice was in the ceiling.

21. Gorrie understood of course, the principle that cold air is heavier than hot air, and that air cooled by the ice would fall down over the patients. In other cases, he had fans blowing over the ice.

22. Nevertheless, the bigger problem as you can imagine was acquiring ice.

23. There were ice companies at the time that sawed huge blocks of ice out of frozen lakes and s hip this ice all over the world.

24. But keep in mind, Florida isn't close to the Northern United States.

25. Ships filled with ice had to sail a long way and well, some ships would encounter storms, or ice would melt and what actually got there was very much in demand, and subsequently not cheap.

26. You have to wonder how much ice would be left, say in August, since it had to be stored all summer in insulated warehouses.

27. So what do you think Gorrie did? Well, with his inclination for tinkering and his science background, he set out to invent an ice making machine! Just imagine!

28. He took advantage of some very basic principles, the most important being this: air that's compressed, cooled and then allowed to re-expand, gets very cold indeed.   

29. And that seems simple, but it's basically the principle all of our subsequent refrigeration technology is based on.

30. Now, Gorrie’s first attempt at a refrigeration machine was big and clumsy. It leaked and broke down a lot, but it did make ice.

31. Unfortunately, although Dr. Gorrie did get a patent for his refrigeration machine in 1851, he never really raised enough money to develop it.

32. He blamed the ice industry for his problems. It's pretty clear that they didn't want to see his invention perfected.

33. Even worse, the media ridiculed his achievement.

34. It's a shame too, because Gorrie was a visionary.

35. He thought this invention could later be adapted to transporting perishable foods all over the country in all seasons among other uses.

36. It's unfortunate nobody took him seriously at the time.
