Step 3：通过听写+跟读，彻底吃透文章内容


听写练习

听写的目的是找到我们的听力盲区，让我们在做跟读的时候能够更有重点。听写时，每个句子播放一般不要超过三遍（特别长的句子除外），如果听完后依然写不全，可以换行写下一句。 听写完成后比对文本，标清楚没听到、没听懂的地方，以及没听懂的原因（例如单词不认识、单词认识但音不熟悉、语音现象造成的听力困难）。 如果时间实在特别紧张，无法完成听写，那么请务必完成下面的跟读练习。


跟读练习


如果实在没有时间做听写，也请务必完成跟读。标注版文本如下，橙色为考点，红色为关键词。



逐句跟读音频+文本

（在这部分，我尽可能保留了原文完整的句子/意群，所以有些音频会比较长，也是为了让大家练习听一个完整意群的能力。如果有困难可以自己在中间断一下。）


1. Listen to part of a lecture in an art history class. 

2.   My topic today is a small oil painting by the 17th century Dutch artist, Johannes Vermeer: ‘Girl with a Pearl Earring.’

3.   It’s signed by the artist, but not dated and it’s probably the most often reproduced of all of Vermeer’s work.

4.   At first, this looks like a portrait, but technically it’s not. It belongs to a category known in Dutch as a ‘tronie.’

5.   Tronie is a category that’s familiar to scholars for many examples by other 17th century Dutch masters.

6.   The subjects of tronies were not intended to be recognized as individuals, but as interesting characters or archetypes, such as the dashing soldier or the wise teacher.

7.   Tronies were usually depicted with just a head and shoulders whereas portraits were normally half-length or full-length and they were much more formally composed.

8.   As you can see, the girl’s wearing a yellow garment and a blue and yellow turban, her earrings, a teardrop-shaped piece of glass that has been varnished to look like a gigantic pearl, and she’s turning to look at us over her left shoulder.

9.   It’s almost like a snapshot. I mean, not a formal photo, but...

10.   Interesting observation John. It does seem spontaneous, but there’s actually painstaking craft on display here.

11.   Notice the way her head’s lit with a highlight on the front of the turban and a reflective light that bounces up from her collar, softly lighting the jaw from below. This lighting scheme's echoed in the earring.

12.   Her lips are slightly open making it possible for the artist to create the most remarkable detail of all.

13.   On the left, a narrow stream of light passes between her lips and strikes the opposite corner of her mouth as a little point of light and then stands out and spreads across her cheek.

14.   This shows Vermeer's powerful observation of optical effects and his amazing ability to translate that observation into paint.

15.   Another point I want to make: something about the girl is illusive somehow. Now why is that?

16.   Well, maybe because there aren’t any walls or furniture telling us where the girl is or her social status or when she lived?

17.   Exactly. No setting, no props, no specifics in her costume. Not even a hairstyle. So, she doesn’t belong to any particular time or place.

18.   I think this illusive, this enigmatic quality is responsible in part for the painting’s popularity.

19.   It reminds me of what people say about the Mona Lisa.

20.   Right! The great Italian Renaissance painter Leonardo da Vinci also created a sense of mystery with Mona Lisa’s hard to interpret smile.

21.   You know, a few years ago a computer analysis was done on Mona Lisa’s face using software that evaluates facial expressions and I believe it found that she was happy, disgusted, fearful, and angry all at once.

22.   Anyway, getting back to our topic. It’s no coincidence that ‘Girl with a Pearl Earring’ has captured the imagination of contemporary writers.

23.   Have any of you read the best-selling novel called Girl with a Pearl Earring or seen the movie adaptation of it? There’s also an opera, partially based on this painting.

24.   Well, all these contemporary works try to supply missing facts to complete our understanding of this image, but despite the painting’s illusive quality, the girl’s features are quite specific, so it’s unlikely that she’s imaginary.

25.   I heard she was Vermeer’s oldest child.

26. Yes, an interesting theory. Um, Vermeer and um, Vermeer and his wife had many children. The eldest, Maria, was probably born in 1654 and ‘Girl with a Pearl Earring’ bears a striking resemblance to a girl in another Vermeer painting, also undated.

27.   The two leading experts on Vermeer are close to agreeing that ‘Pearl Earring’ was painted when Maria would have been between 10 and 12.

28.   Those same scholars believe the other painting was made a year later, but keep in mind that these dates are based solely on the expert’s opinions of where these paintings fit into the development of Vermeer’s style.

29.   In my view, the style of these paintings could fit just as well into when Maria was a couple of years older.

30.   But, how does any of this prove that the girl pictured was Maria?

31.   After Vermeer’s death, his wife had to sell his artwork to pay bills, but she tried to keep the other painting I just referred to in her family.

32.   I suspect it was because it included an image of one of her children.
