Listen to part of a lecture in an anthropology class. 

 

1）We're going to talk about some of the changes that occurred in human societies as a result of people becoming sedentary(定居的). (1)

我们将讨论人类社会因定居而发生的一些变化.

 

2） Living a settled(定居) lifestyle in villages or cities. 

也就是在乡村或城市进行定居。（sedentary有点难，所以解释一句。）


3）[Now, before humans became sedentary, how did they live?] Noah? 

在人类定居之前，他们是如何生活的？Noah？

 

4）In nomadic bands(游牧部族), hunting animals and gathering fruit, seeds and other plants, right? 

以游牧部落的形式生活, 猎杀动物，采集水果、种子和其他植物，对吗？

 

5）Yes, and people have been living as hunter gatherers for tens of thousands of years. Sedentism, the settled lifestyle, is relatively new. 

没错，人们作为狩猎采集者生活了数万年。定居是一种相对较新的固定生活方式。

 

6）[Now,] the transition from hunter-gatherer societies to settled agricultural societies is called the Neolithic Revolution. 

从打猎采集者社会向定居的农业社会的过渡被称为新石器时代革命。

 

7）Sometimes it's called The Agricultural Revolution, since this is when people learned how to raise animals and tend(照料) crops full time.

有时也被称为农业革命，因为正是这个时候人们学会如何全职饲养动物和照料农作物。

 

8） This neolithic revolution started around 10000 years ago.

这场新石器时代革命大约始于10000年前。


9）But it's important to realize that this was not a sudden change.

需要注意的是这并不是一个突然的变化。

 

10） It didn't happen all at once everywhere at the same time.

这种变化也并不是在所有地方都同时发生的。

 

11） In fact, it took place over the course of many generations, hundreds or or thousands of years. 

事实上，这个转变的过程是花了很长的时间，跨越了很多代（才完成）。

 

12）The first established human settlements were in the Middle East.

最早建立的人类定居点位于中东地区。

 

13）But in Western Europe, for example, the transition(过渡) to a sedentary lifestyle doesn't begin until much later, about 3000 years ago. (2) 

但在西欧，朝着定居生活方式的过渡直到3000年前才开始。

 

14）And some groups never even made the transition, so there are people who even today are still living as hunter gatherers. 

一些群体甚至从未经历过这种转变，因此，有些人甚至在今天仍然以狩猎采集的方式生活。

 

15）Yes. Noah. 

Why would they still be hunter gatherers? They don't know about agriculture? 

Noah.（你想说啥？）

为什么他们仍然是狩猎采集者？他们不知道农业的存在吗？（不知有汉？）

 

16）Oh, they know about it, all right. That's not the issue.(6)

他们是知道的。这不是他们没有从事农业的原因。

 

17）The fact is, there are plenty of good reasons not to settle down, to remain hunter- gatherers. 

事实是，他们有很多很好的理由继续维持打猎采集的生活方式，而不是定居下来。

 

18）Really? Like what?

[Okay. Let's back up.] Let's talk first about the advantages of sedentism. 

是吗？比如啥理由？

这样吧，让我们退一步解释一下。让我们先聊聊定居的优势。

 

19）What gained by living, say in a village? Abbey? 

比如说住在一个村子里生活会有什么好处呢？（定居的一种形式）Abbey？

 

20）A Reliable supply of food, like from raising livestock(牲畜) and farming. 

（获得）可靠的食物供应，比如通过饲养牲畜，还有农业种植。

 

21）Living in a village, growing food, people could store up more food than they consumed.(消耗)

住在一个村庄里，种植粮食，人们可以储存比他们吃掉的更多的粮食。（库里有粮，心里不慌。）

 

22）Good, and that was useful when the weather turned against them. 

很好，当天气不好的时候，食物储备就很有用了。

 

23）OK, what other advantages? Are there any social advantages to sedentism?

好的，还有什么其他优势吗? 定居的生活方式有什么社会方面的优势吗？

 

24）Specialization of labor(劳动分工)?

Go on.

分工协作？

展开讲讲。

 

25）Well, with a surplus of food, some members of a village could specialize in something besides farming like making pottery or weaving or creating governments. Things like that. 

有了多余的食物，村里的一些人可以专门从事农业以外的事情，比如制作陶器、编织或创建政府。诸如此类的事情。

 

26）Good. Now to be sure the neolithic revolution made civilization possible. 

好的现在可以肯定的是，新石器时代的革命使文明成为可能。

 

27）Buildings, writing, government, probably none of this could have happened until humans figured out how to control crops to ensure a steady food supply(食物供给). 

建筑，写作，政府，也许这些都不会出现，直到人类发现如何控制作物以确保稳定的粮食供应.

 

28）[However, ] there are significant disadvantages to living in a settled society, and there is much to be said for being a hunter gatherer. 

然而，生活在一个定居社会中也有着巨大的劣势，作为一个狩猎采集者也有很多好处。

 

29）Like what? 

Better health for one, the hunter gatherer lifestyle is significantly healthier than a sedentary one. 

比如什么？

比如更健康，狩猎采集者的生活方式比定居的生活方式要健康得多。


30）Why is that? You'd think that with a steady or food supply, people would be healthier. 

为什么呢？有了稳定的食物供应，人们才会更健康不是更理所当然的事情吗？

 

31）You'd think so. But keep in mind that in settled societies, people generally raise only a few kinds of crops(农作物), 

你可能会这么觉得。（你觉得的都不对）但请记住，在定居社会中，人们通常只种植几种农作物。

 

32）wheat, barley or a particular fruit. A diet of only two or three kinds of foods translates into bad nutrition(营养). (3-A)

小麦、大麦或某种水果。只有两到三种食物的饮食会导致营养不良.

 

33）Hunter-gatherers’ diet(饮食结构), on the other hand, consists of all different kinds of food, much more nutritious.

另一方面，狩猎采集者的饮食结构由各种各样的食物组成，这些食物更有营养。

 

34） A recent study of prehistoric skeletons in Europe found that this poor nutrition led to, for example, a decrease in peoples height over time. 

最近一项对欧洲史前骨骼的研究发现，这种营养不良导致人们的身高随着时间的推移而下降。

 

35）The study shows that males were, on average, about seven centimeters shorter than they had been before the rise of agriculture and permanent settlements. (5-C) 

这项研究表明，男性平均比农业和永久定居点兴起之前矮7厘米左右。（懂了，农业使人变矮。）

 

36）The skeletons also showed that there were more diseases and more dental problems among settled people. 

这些骨骼还表明定居人口中有更多的疾病和更多的牙齿问题。

 

37）Why they get sick more? 

为什么定居的人更容易生病呢？

 

38）[One reason] is that people in early villages lived in very close proximity to their farm animals and to each other.

其中一个原因是早期村庄的人们生活在离农场动物非常近的地方，人们彼此之间住得也非常近。

 

 39）And this led to unsanitary(不卫生的) conditions where disease could easily spread. 

这导致了不卫生的，易于疾病传播的条件。

 

40）Uh-huh. [Also,] nomads enjoyed greater social freedoms, had more time on their hands. 

此外，游牧民族享有更大的社会自由，有更多的时间。

 

41）A farmer would toil from sun up to sundown.But a hunter gatherer would maybe work just four hours a day to get their food and the rest of their time would be spent socializing and resting. (3-C)

一个农民会从早到晚劳作。但是一个狩猎采集者可能每天只工作四个小时来获取食物，剩下的时间将用于社交和休息。（别说了，那边还招人吗？）

 

42）[So why would people even want to settle down then? ]

那人们为啥要定居下来呢？（既然打猎采集有如此多的好处的话。）

 

43）Well, that study of skeletons. It shows that people who have made the transition to sedentism and were living in villages suffered less bone trauma, fewer broken bones. 

对骨骼的研究。这表明，那些定居的人类，也就是生活在村庄的人，遭受的骨创伤更少，骨折也更少。

 

44）Which suggests that despite being less healthy, it will probably safer than hunter-gatherer. (5-D)

这表明，尽管它不太健康，但它可能比打猎采集者更安全。（活着比啥都强？）
