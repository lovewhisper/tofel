文本：


Listen to part of a lecture in an archaeology class. 


Professor: We've been talking about techniques for locating and dating archaeological artifacts. Now, in some places, archaeological investigations are difficult, and we have to use some special techniques. In the country of Iceland, for example. (1)Now, Iceland is a volcanic island located in the North Atlantic ocean, and about 10% of it is covered by glaciers. 

教授：我们一直在讨论考古文物的定位和年代测定技术。现在，在一些地方，考古调查很困难，我们必须使用一些特殊的技术。例如，在冰岛。冰岛是一个位于北大西洋的火山岛，约有10%被冰川覆盖。（没什么用的背景介绍）


Male Student: So is it too cold to work there? Or maybe everything is under the ice? 

男学生：是太冷了没法做研究吗？还是一切都被冰层盖住了？


Professor: No, that's not the problem. It's that Iceland has virtually no trees to hold down soil. So there's been a lot of erosion, especially erosion of soil from the highlands to lower coastal areas. And this erosion has buried much of Iceland under deep deposits of soil. And remember what I said about few trees? With so little wood available, the earliest dwellings in Iceland were built mainly from compressed, peat.

教授：都不是。这是因为冰岛几乎没有树木来保持土壤。所以侵蚀很严重，特别是从高地到低海岸地区的土壤侵蚀。这种侵蚀将冰岛的大部分地区埋在了深厚的土壤中。还记得我说这里树木很少吗？（您不刚说完么。。。）因为木材太少了，冰岛最早的住宅主要是用压缩的泥炭建造的。


Male Student: Did you say peat? 

男学生：你是说泥炭吗？


Professor: Yes, peat. That's a light kind of airy soil that comes from bogs and wetlands and contains a high proportion of decayed organic matter. Anyway, the peat can be compressed and dried and made into big, thick blocks. That's what the walls of the early Icelandic houses were made of, mostly. Now, since the walls of these old houses were made basically of a kind of soil, they're really hard to locate underground, cause the material these buried structures are made of doesn't differ very much from the soil that surrounds them. (2) 

教授：是的。这是一种轻质的疏松的土壤，来自沼泽和湿地，含有大量腐烂的有机物。泥炭可以被压缩和干燥，制成又大又厚的块。大部分早期冰岛房屋的墙壁都是由这种材料制成的。现在，由于这些老房子的墙壁本质上是由一种土壤制成的，它们很难在地下被找到，因为这些地下结构所用的材料与周围的土壤差别不大。


Female Student: So how do you find these peat walls buried in all that other soil? 

女学生：那我们是如何发现这些埋在其他土壤中的泥炭墙的呢？


Professor: One way is to borrow a modern technique used by geophysicists. 

教授：一种方法是借用地球物理学家使用的现代技术。


Male Student: Sorry if you said this already, but what are they looking for in Iceland anyway? (6)

男学生：对不起，如果您已经讲过了的话，但他们在冰岛到底在找什么呢？


Professor: Ah, yes, good question. Iceland, and stories about it. Icelandic sagas have intrigued people for centuries. (3) The sagas seemed to be historical accounts of actual norse explorers and settlers. But since we've always lacked hard evidence to support the truth of the stories, they seem to be more like legends. There's always been a draw, though, almost like a calling for some of us, to verify the truth of these stories, historians, astronomers, navigators, they've all tried to find proofs that people settled where the stories indicate. And we archaeologists have to, by trying to locate dwellings, evidence of animal domestication or farming or iron smelting, any signs of early settlement.

教授：好问题。冰岛，以及关于它的故事。几个世纪以来，冰岛传奇一直吸引着人们。这些传说似乎是关于挪威探险家和定居者的历史记载。但由于我们一直缺乏确凿的证据来支持故事的真实性，它们似乎更像是传说。不过，冰岛总有一种神奇的吸引力，呼吁我们中的一些人来验证这些故事的真实性，历史学家、天文学家、航海家，他们都试图找到证据，证明人们定居在故事表明的地方。我们考古学家必须通过寻找住所、动物驯化或农耕或炼铁的证据，找到早期定居的迹象。


Male Student: Cool. Are you talking about like the time of the Vikings? 

男学生：酷。你说的是维京人时代吗？


Professor: Yes, that's exactly it. One of these Icelandic sagas tells of some viking explorers who were probably the first Europeans to cross the Atlantic and live in North America.

教授：是的，没错。其中一个冰岛传奇讲述了一些维京探险家的故事，他们可能是第一批穿越大西洋生活在北美的欧洲人。


Female Student: North America? But I thought you were talking about Iceland. 

女学生：北美？但我以为你说的是冰岛。


Professor: I am. See, the story tells of a Viking family from Iceland called Thorfinnson, who settled in North America for a few years, but then moved back to Iceland, which is not that far away. So there is a great interest in investigating Viking era sites in Iceland, especially in the place where the saga says this family finally settled. 

教授：是的。你看，这个故事讲述了一个来自冰岛的维京人家庭，名叫T，在北美定居了几年，然后又搬回了不远的冰岛。因此，人们对调查冰岛的维京人时代遗址非常感兴趣，尤其是传说中这个家族最终定居的地方。


But back to my point, one team of archaeologists working there decided to use an electromagnetic remote sensing tool to try to locate buried structures. Now, this remote sensing tool, which as I mentioned before, is usually used by geophysicists. This tool can distinguish between different materials that look the same to the eye, but have different compositions.

但回到我之前说的内容，一个在那里工作的考古学家团队决定使用电磁遥感工具来定位被埋藏的建筑结构。正如我之前提到的，这个遥感工具通常被地球物理学家使用。该工具可以区分肉眼看起来相同但成分不同的不同材质。


  Here's how it works. Regular soil conducts electricity, but walls made of peat do not conduct electricity well. So the tool sends down alternating currents of electricity, and then measures how well the electric current travels through the ground in different places. Then you look at all of your data, look for patterns of electrical resistance, and this reveals where walls are located. (4) 

下面是它的工作原理。普通土壤导电，但泥炭墙导电性不好。所以，遥感设备会发出交流电，然后测量电流在不同地方通过土层的传输情况。然后查看所有数据，查找电阻（差异）的规律，这将揭示(被埋在地底的)墙的位置。


So, anyway, the team wanted to investigate a site in Iceland that looks like the place where the old saga says that Thorfinnsons built their home. So they used this technique, and found the remains of a large farmhouse there that they think probably did belong to that Thorfinnson family. Because when they dated the building, it corresponded exactly to when that Thorfinnson family should have been there according to the sagas. (5) 

所以，不管怎样，这个团队想调查冰岛的一个地方，这个地方看起来就像传说中T建造家园的地方。因此，他们利用这项技术，在那里发现了一座大型农舍的遗迹，他们认为这座农舍可能属于T家族。因为当他们确定这座建筑的年代时，它与传说中托芬森家族应该在那里的时间完全一致。(5)


Now the team is working to find other evidence, especially personal artifacts, to show that it really was that family who lived there. Everyone had always thought that Thorfinnson house might be in that area, though probably right underneath a nearby museum, where it might have been damaged during museum construction. But in fact, this Viking era structure was located in a field behind the museum buried just below the surface. Thank goodness for the remote sensing tool, or this house might never have been found. 

现在，研究小组正在寻找其他证据，尤其是个人物品，以证明确实是那个家庭住在那里。人们都一直认为Thorfinnson的房子可能就在那个地区，尽管它可能就在附近一家博物馆的正下方，在博物馆建设过程中可能已经被损坏了。但事实上，这座维京时代的建筑位于博物馆后面的一块地里，就在离地表不远的地方。还好遥感工具，否则这所房子可能永远也找不到了。
