Listen to part of a lecture in an Astronomy class

 

1）(male professor) The surface of Mars today is mostly very cold, certainly too cold for there to be liquid water present.

今天的火星表面通常很冷，冷到不可能有液态水存在。


2）Yet, scientists have speculated for decades that in its early history, about four billion years ago, Mars was much warmer and wetter than it is today. (1) 

然而，几十年来，科学家们一直推测，在火星的早期历史中，大约40亿年前，火星比今天温暖湿润得多。


3）Observations from Earth and from space have detected features in the Martian landscape indicating that Mars once had liquid water on its surface,

从地球和太空进行的观测发现了火星地貌的特征，表明火星表面曾经有液态水，


4）features like interconnected valleys that appear to have been formed by rivers, （2-A) 

这些特征像是由河流形成的相互连接的山谷，


5）And also depressions(凹地), low lying areas filled with dust and sediment that look like ancient lake beds. (2-C)

还有凹地, 即布满灰尘和沉积物低洼地区，看起来像古老的湖床。


6）[So, what might account for(解释) this warm wet Mars?]

那么，怎么解释火星曾经温暖潮湿的环境的？


7）Well, one hypothesis suggests that Mars' atmosphere once had very high levels of carbon dioxide. 

嗯，有一种假设认为火星的大气层曾经有很高的二氧化碳含量。


8）OK, let's take a step back for a minute. 

好，我们退一步解释一下。


9）We know that carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas and that greenhouse gases capture(捕获) solar energy that would otherwise radiate back into space from a planet's surface. 

我们知道二氧化碳是一种温室气体，它可以捕获原本会从行星表面辐射回太空的太阳能。


10）So, these greenhouse gases trap that energy within the planet's lower atmosphere and keep the surface of the planet warm. 

这些温室气体将热量困在地球大气的低层，并使地球表面保持温暖。


11）Perhaps warm enough for water to exist there in liquid form like it does on Earth.

也许足够温暖到让水可以像地球上一样以液态形式存在。


12）[But some astronomers remained skeptical.] 

但一些天文学家仍持怀疑态度。


13）You see, another thing about carbon dioxide is that it is easily absorbed into water  

二氧化碳的另一个特点是它很容易被水吸收，


14） and eventually the carbon dioxide in the water may combine with other elements in the water to form minerals called carbonates. 

最终水中的二氧化碳可能会与水中的其他元素结合，形成称为碳酸盐的矿物质。


15）Carbonates then settle to the bottom of these bodies of water and form deposits there, 

然后碳酸盐沉淀到这些水体的底部，在那里形成沉积物，


16）chalk-like layers that can remain long after the water itself is gone. 

一层在水干之后还可以保留很长时间的粉笔状的沉积物。


17）So, if the hypothesis is correct, if the atmosphere of Mars was once rich in carbon dioxide,

因此，如果这个假设是正确的，如果火星的大气层曾经富含二氧化碳的话，


18） there should be now huge deposits of carbonate on the planet. 

那么现在火星上应该有大量的碳酸盐沉积物。


19）But nobody was able to detect that much carbonate there，using the standard method for studying the chemical composition(化学成分) of a planet: spectroscopy. (3)

但是，我们没能用研究行星化学成分的标准研究方法，即光谱学，在那里检测出足够量的碳酸盐。


20）Astronomers use spectroscopy to study the color spectrum(光谱) of light, in this case, the light being reflected from planets. 

天文学家利用光谱学研究彩色光谱，研究行星的时候，我们研究的是他们反射的光。


21）By analyzing the reflected light, researchers can determine, among other things, the chemical composition of a planet. 

通过分析反射光，研究人员可以确定行星的化学成分。（重复）


22）And a detailed spectroscopic analysis of light reflected from the surface of Mars did not detect huge amounts of carbonate. 

对火星表面反射光的详细光谱分析并没有检测到大量的碳酸盐。（又是重复，结论讲过了。）


23）It found less than one-third of the carbonate expected to be present, if a carbon dioxide-rich atmosphere had, in fact, once existed there.

我们只探测到了不足预期量三分之一的碳酸盐存在。（无尽的重复。）


24）[So, if we are not seeing lots of carbonate, would we be right to reject this hypothesis?]

如果我们没有检测到大量的碳酸盐，是不是就可以推翻这个假设了呢？


25）Or could there be other explanations? 

或者有其他解释？


26）Well, maybe it's just much less simple than we thought. 

也许事情比我们想象的要更复杂。


27）For example, we've detected a lot of sulfates on Mars.

例如，我们在火星上发现了许多硫酸盐。


28）This means the planet's atmosphere once contained a great deal of sulfur in various forms. 

这意味着火星的大气层曾经含有大量不同形式的硫。


29）And we know that atmospheric sulfur and carbon dioxide interact in all sorts of complex ways. 

我们知道大气中的硫和二氧化碳以各种复杂的方式相互作用。


30）So that may help explain why we've detected so much less carbonate than anticipated. (4)

因此，这可能有助于解释为什么我们检测到的碳酸盐比预期的要少得多。


31）[And there's another issue. ] Spectroscopic analysis of reflected light only detects materials on the planet's surface.(5) 

还有另一个问题。反射光的光谱分析只能检测行星表面的物质。


32）But what if the carbonates are mixed together with dust and other sediments(沉积物) to form much less concentrated deposits，

但如果碳酸盐与灰尘和其他沉积物混合在一起形成浓度低得多的矿物质沉淀呢？


33）Or covered over and buried just under the surface. 

或者被覆盖并埋在地表之下。


34）Spectroscopic analysis wouldn't detect those carbonates. 

光谱分析将无法检测出这些碳酸盐。


35）So, we needed to gather evidence in other ways like from spacecraft that have actually landed on Mars. 

所以，我们需要以其他方式收集证据，比如用登陆火星的航天器收集证据。


36）And, in fact, instruments on a spacecraft that landed near Mars' North Pole confirmed that carbonates are present just beneath the surface there, 

事实上，在火星北极附近着陆的航天器上的仪器证实火星表面下方确实存在碳酸盐，


37）which seems to support the idea of early carbonate formation on Mars.

这似乎支持了火星早期碳酸盐形成的观点。


38）[The problem here,] though, is that the instruments couldn't measure the amount of carbonate present in the rocks. 

不过，问题是这些仪器无法测量岩石中碳酸盐的含量。


39）However, a recent analysis of data from one of the Mars rovers did result in an accurate measurement of the amount of carbonate in some rocks jutting up from the surface of Mars in an area called the Columbia Hills. 

然而，最近我们对一辆火星登陆车的数据进行的分析后，确实得出了在一个叫做CH地区的火星表面突出的一些岩石中的碳酸盐含量。（就是测出来了，怎么测的，尤其是在哪测的，通常不那么重要。）


40）The rover determined that the rocks averaged around twenty five percent carbonate.

通过登陆车的数据，我们确定了这些岩石的平均碳酸盐含量约为25%。


41）If such a high carbonate content were to prove common elsewhere on Mars，

如果碳酸盐高含量在火星上其他地方也很普遍的话，


42）that'll be strong support for high levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide in Mars' distant past. (6)

将有力地支持火星遥远的过去大气中有大量的二氧化碳。


43）If not, if that rock was just an isolated occurrence, well, the search and the debate continue.

如果碳酸盐高含量并非普遍情况，如果那块岩石只是一个孤立的事件，那么，搜寻和辩论仍将继续。

 





Listen to part of a lecture in an Astronomy class


(male professor) The surface of Mars today is mostly very cold, certainly too cold for there to be liquid water present. Yet, scientists have speculated for decades that in its early history, about four billion years ago, Mars was much warmer and wetter than it is today. (1) Observations from Earth and from space have detected features in the Martian landscape indicating that Mars once had liquid water on its surface, features like interconnected valleys that appear to have been formed by rivers (2-A) and also depressions(凹地), low lying areas filled with dust and sediment that look like ancient lake beds. (2-C)


[So, what might account for(解释) this warm wet Mars?] Well, one hypothesis suggests that Mars' atmosphere once had very high levels of carbon dioxide. OK, let's take a step back for a minute. We know that carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas and that greenhouse gases capture(捕获) solar energy that would otherwise radiate back into space from a planet's surface. So, these greenhouse gases that energy within the planet's lower atmosphere and keep the surface of the planet warm. Perhaps warm enough for water to exist there in liquid form like it does on Earth.


[But some astronomers remained skeptical.] You see another thing about carbon dioxide is that it is easily absorbed into water and eventually the carbon dioxide in the water may combine with other elements in the water to form minerals called carbonates. Carbonates then settle to the bottom of these bodies of water and form deposits there, chalk-like layers that can remain long after the water itself is gone. So, if the hypothesis is correct, if the atmosphere of Mars was once rich in carbon dioxide, there should be now huge deposits of carbonate on the planet. But nobody was able to detect that much carbonate there using the standard method for studying the chemical composition(化学成分) of a planet: spectroscopy. (3)


Astronomers use spectroscopy to study the color spectrum(光谱) of light, in this case, the light being reflected from planets. By analyzing the reflected light, researchers can determine, among other things, the chemical composition of a planet. And a detailed spectroscopic analysis of light reflected from the surface of Mars did not detect huge amounts of carbonate. It found less than one-third of the carbonate expected to be present, if a carbon dioxide-rich atmosphere had, in fact, once existed there.


[So, if we are not seeing lots of carbonate, would we be right to reject this hypothesis?] Or could there be other explanations? Well, maybe it's just much less simple than we thought. For example, we've detected a lot of sulfates on Mars. This means the planet's atmosphere once contained a great deal of sulfur in various forms. And we know that atmospheric sulfur and carbon dioxide interact in all sorts of complex ways. So that may help explain why we've detected so much less carbonate than anticipated. (4)


[And there's another issue. ] Spectroscopic analysis of reflected light only detects materials on the planet's surface. (5) But what if the carbonates are mixed together with dust and other sediments(沉积物) to form much less concentrated deposits? Or covered over and buried just under the surface. Spectroscopic analysis wouldn't detect those carbonates. So, we needed to gather evidence in other ways like from spacecraft that have actually landed on Mars. And, in fact, instruments on a spacecraft that landed near Mars' North Pole confirmed that carbonates are present just beneath the surface there, which seems to support the idea of early carbonate formation on Mars.


[The problem here,] though, is that the instruments couldn't measure the amount of carbonate present in the rocks. However, a recent analysis of data from one of the Mars rovers did result in an accurate measurement of the amount of carbonate in some rocks jutting up from the surface of Mars in an area called the Columbia Hills. The rover determined that the rocks averaged around twenty five percent carbonate. If such a high carbonate content were to prove common elsewhere on Mars that'll be strong support for high levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide in Mars' distant past. (6) If not, if that rock was just an isolated occurrence, well, the search and the debate continue.
