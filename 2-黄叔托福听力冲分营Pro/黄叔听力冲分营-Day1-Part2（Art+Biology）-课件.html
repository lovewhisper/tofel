文本：


Listen to part of a lecture in a biology class. 


Professor: I think the lens of the eyes just about the most fascinating part of the body. 

我认为眼睛的晶状体是人体中最神奇的部分。


For one, it's the only, I repeat, only transparent cellular tissue in the human body.

首先，它是人体中唯一的透明细胞组织。


In fact, truly transparent tissue is very uncommon in nature. 

事实上，真正透明的组织在自然界中是很不常见的。


There are some sort of clear creatures that live in oceans and rivers, like the jellyfish.

海洋和河流中生活着一些的透明生物，如水母。


Their quasi-transparency allows them to blend in with the water to escape predators.

它们的类透明属性使它们能够与水融为一体以逃避捕食者。


 I say quasi, because jellyfish are translucent. They're not totally see through like the lens is. (2)

我说类似，是因为水母是半透明的。它们并不像晶体那样完全透明。


So you have to wonder why the lens is, how is transparent tissue possible? (1) Marlene?

所以你肯定会想"为什么晶状体是透明的呢？怎么会有透明的组织？“马林？


Female Student: There's no direct blood supply to that part of the eye. (3-C)

眼睛的那一部分没有直接的血液供应。


Professor: Good. Exactly. Marlene. The blood and blood vessels would color the lens, the lens would probably end up looking pinkish, but that's only part of the explanation. And what else? 

教授：很好。完全正确。血液和血管会给晶状体着色，最后可能会看起来是粉红色的，但这只是一部分原因。那还有什么其他的因素呢？


Male Student: There was something about crystals and fibers in the chapter we read.

嗯，我们读的那一章里有关于晶体和纤维的内容。


It said that the lens is sometimes called a biological crystal, not a true crystal, like a diamond, but it has a very regular arrangement of cells. 

它说，晶状体有时被称为生物晶体，不是像钻石那样的真正的晶体，但它有非常规则的细胞排列。


The cells are exactly aligned and tightly compacted. 

细胞整齐地排列并紧密地结合在一起。


Professor: Right. It's made up of layers of cells, but the cells are all of one type, a rigid elongated cell, that's called a lens fiber. 

教授：对。它是由一层层的细胞组成的，但这些细胞都是一种类型，一种刚性的细长细胞。这就是所谓的晶状体纤维。


Because it's all one kind of cell, (3-E) and because they're aligned so regularly, you don't have all the angles you get when different types of cells are fitted together, which refract light, bend it as it passes through, and that contributes to its transparency. 

因为它都是同一种细胞，而且因为它们是如此有规律地排列，所以它不会出现当不同类型的细胞排列在一起时出现的参差不齐的情况。参差不齐会让光通过时产生折射。消除这种参差不齐的情况有助于增加透明度。


But there's something missing in a lens fiber. What doesn't a lens fiber have? 

但是晶体纤维中缺少一些东西。晶状体纤维不具备什么？


Male Student: We said blood vessels, right? 

学生。嗯，我们说过血管，对吗？


Professor: The lens itself, right. Is that it? Anybody? No? This is very important. The lens fibers don't have organelles.(6) 

教授。晶状体缺少血管。（请搞清楚概念朋友）还有其他的回答吗？有人能回答吗？真的没有了？（别挣扎了，确实没有人理你。）还有非常重要的一点，晶状体纤维没有细胞器。


Organelles are parts that function like miniature organs, specialized parts of a cell, like the nucleus that stores the DNA or mitochondria, which are the powerhouse of a cell. Okay?

细胞器的功能就像微型器官，是细胞中有专门功能的组件，像储存DNA的细胞核还有作为细胞的动力源线粒体都属于细胞器。。


Now what's interesting here is that lens fibers, as their first forming, they start out as cells with organelles. But as the eye develops, the cells lose all of them, including the nuclei.

有意思的是晶状体纤维在最初形成时是带有细胞器的。但随着眼睛的发育，细胞失去了所有的细胞器，包括细胞核。


And without organelles, the lens fiber is nice and clear, and all those lens fibers packed together make a nifty crystal clear lens.

在没有细胞器的情况下，晶状体纤维是漂亮而清晰的。所有这些晶状体纤维挤在一起，形成了一个漂亮的水晶般清晰的晶状体。


But that clarity comes at a cost. So, Marlene, what's the downside of this? 

但这种清晰度是有代价的。Marlene，这有什么坏处呢？

 

Female Student: Actually, if it doesn't have organelles, I mean DNA or whatever, Then what happens if the lens is damaged or something? 

学生：如果它没有细胞器，DNA之类的，那么如果晶状体被损坏的话会怎么样呢？


Professor: And that's the problem.(4) The price of clear vision of transparent lenses is that the lens fibers can't regenerate or repair themselves as other cells do. Which leads me to this. 

教授: 这就是问题所在，透明晶状体的清晰视觉的代价是，晶状体纤维不能像其他细胞那样自我再生或修复，这让我想到了这么一个问题。


As we get older, the lens can get cloudy or yellow. It can be cataracts or happen just from aging.

随着我们年龄的增长，晶状体会变得浑浊或发黄。这可能是白内障，也可能只是因为老化而发生。


There are lots of possible causes of that, but when you get down to it, the fact remains that the lens fibers just can't repair themselves.

有很多可能的原因，但当你深入了解后，根本原因还是晶状体纤维不能自我修复。


So when the lens gets discolored, what do you think happens to your sight? 

因此，当晶状体变色时，你认为会对你的视力产生什么影响？


Male Student: Would change your color perception wouldn't it?


嗯，会改变你的色觉？


Professor: Exactly, discolored lenses absorb certain wavelengths of light? So some light rays won't reach the retina. 

没错，变色的晶状体只吸收某些波长的光线。所以有些光线不会到达视网膜，


So some wavelengths aren't processed, which will affect your color vision. 

也就是说有些波长的光线不会被处理，这会影响你的色觉。


I just read an article about the French impressionist painter Claude Monet. (5) And it was really interesting because it showed examples of his paintings.

我刚刚读了一篇关于法国印象派画家克劳德-莫奈的文章。它真的很有趣，因为它展示了他的画作的例子。


And you could see from his later paintings what he was seeing, how his eyes, his eyesight had changed. 

你可以从他后来的画作中看到他看到了什么，他的眼睛和他的视力是如何变化的。


As he got older, certain colors began to dominate his work, yellows and reds and browns.

随着他年龄的增长，某些颜色开始主宰他的作品，黄色、红色和棕色。



He was mostly seeing those colors because his lenses had yellowed and weren't transparent anymore. 

他之所以能看到这些颜色，主要是因为他的晶体已经发黄，不再透明了。
