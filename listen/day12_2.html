文本：

Listen to a conversation between a student and an employee at the financial services office. 


Employee: Hi, how can I help you? 

Student: Hi, my name's Arne. This is my first semester here, and I'm hoping you might be able to help me. See, I don't know how to put this. But I'm having some trouble managing my money. 

Employee: You mean you didn't pay your tuition bill? 

Student: No, I'm okay with the big stuff. I have student loans that pay for most of the tuition, but it's the everyday things that add up… (1) 

Employee: You mean like books food, things like that.

Student: My parents give me an allowance for that stuff, but it's just not enough. I've asked them to increase it, even a little, but they think I need to learn to manage on my own. (2)

Employee: That's a really common problem with first year students. You've come to the right place, we have resources that can help. So, first question. Do you have a budget worked out for your spending? 

Student: No, I don't. 

Employee: Okay. I can send you a budget worksheet. It's a computer document. And it will help you think through what your expenses are and where you can cut costs. You'll be surprised at how much the little things add up. You know, if you spend $2 on a cup of coffee each morning, that's almost $200 a semester. (3)

Student: So if I could make my own coffee, that would be great, but I can't, since I live in the dorm true. 

Employee: True, But that's just an example. I also have this other sheet with suggestions. It's just a list of money saving ideas, like by used books instead of new ones, and buy a bike since that's cheaper than taking the bus or owning a car. (4-B)

Student: I'd love to get a bike. Then I could even get a job in town. 

Employee: If you are thinking about a job, we do have postings for jobs as well. They're on the bulletin board outside my office.

Student: Yea, I saw it on my way in it's empty. 

Employee: Yes, the positions get filled pretty quickly. But new one's open up too. I, I can't guarantee anything, but if something becomes available, that's where you're going to find out about it. (5)

Student: Well, if I got a job, I certainly have a little more spending money that would show my parents I'm managing on my own. 

Employee: But what's your course load this semester? If you have more than four courses, you might want to work on your budget and monitor your spending, rather than get a job for now. 

Student: I have four courses, but one is biology, and there's a 3 hour lab each week. So it's really like a fifth course. 

Employee: You ought to think about it then. Maybe try to work on your budget and spending for a month, and see how you doing, if you are still strapped for cash, but you're keeping up with all your course work, then you could try to find a job. (4-C)

Student: Yeah, and maybe by then that bulletin board won't be empty.

文本：


Listen to a conversation between a student and a receptionist at the housing office.



Student: Hi. You know how you advertise the refrigerator rentals last week？


Receptionist: Yeah. We put up a bunch of ads in the residence halls, microwaves, fridges, VC Rs and TVs.


Student: Oh, you have television rentals, too. I wish I'd known. It would have been better to rent one than buying one. Well, too late now.


Receptionist: I'm sorry, but I think it's a good deal if you need it for a semester. But I have to say the fridge rental is probably the most popular rental for people, you know, who live in a tiny, single room, which is just a bed and a desk. 


Student: Totally. I usually eat in the dining hall, but once in a while, I'd like to cook in my room. Maybe I'll look into renting a microwave.


Receptionist: Ok, I'll get you their info. The deadline for microwaves isn’t until next month. 


Student: Great. Anyway though, I signed up for a fridge last week.


Receptionist: All right. Well, is there a problem with it?


Student: Actually, I still haven't gotten it.


Receptionist: Ok, this is the first year we’ve used this rental company, so we're working out a few kinks we've had with them.


Student: I thought it was gonna be delivered yesterday. 


Receptionist: But…


Student: So I waited until like 3 o'clock and it never came. So I just wanted to know if I missed it. And if not…


Receptionist: Wait. Who said anything about delivery? You were supposed to pick it up yesterday. I didn't you see the long line of students waiting to pick up refrigerators in front of your building?


Student: Seriously? I guess I just assumed that someone's gonna bring it to my room. I mean it's a fridge. 


Receptionist: Yeah, but it's not that heavy since it's one of those portable ones.


Student: And I thought I gave them my room number when I ordered it.


Receptionist: I saw the form, it just asked for name and phone number.


Student: Ok, well, should I, can I pick it up now?


Receptionist: Well, we don't keep them here. The rental company brought them yesterday, and we could only schedule them for one day. So, if you still want one, the best bet is to go there and try to get one.


Student: Ok, where's the rental place?


Receptionist: It's not too far from here. I can give you their number and address. But you might want to call them before you go and waste a trip.


Student: Well, they should still have my name on the list. Don't you think?


Receptionist: Yeah, but you haven't paid or anything yet, right? Because you didn't show up yesterday and all, so they might have thought you changed your mind and let someone else take it. I don't know, but you wanna make sure before you go up there.


Student: You're right. I'll call him right now.
