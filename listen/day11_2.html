Listen to part of a lecture in an astronomy class. 

 

1) I'd like to go over some points from the reading you did about solar wind. Can someone tell me what solar wind(太阳风) is? 

我想回顾一下你读到的关于太阳风的一些事情。有人能告诉我什么是太阳风吗？

 

2) Uh, it’s clouds of electrically charged particles(带电粒子) that are constantly being emitted(散发) by the sun in a spiral because of the sun's rotation(自转).        

 太阳风是一团带电粒子，它不断地被太阳以螺旋状往外散发，因为太阳的自转。

 

3）That's right, streams of particles emanate(散发) from the sun's upper atmosphere as a result of reactions that take place within the sun. 

没错，由于太阳内部发生的反应而从太阳的上层大气中散发出的粒子流。（其实就是重复和补充一下学生的回答。）


4）[Professor, why is it called solar wind? ]

教授，为什么我们叫它太阳风？

 

5）In many ways It behaves like wind. On earth, wind movement is caused by differences in air pressure between one region and another. 

它的行为方式和风有很多相似之处。在地球上，风的运动是由一个地区和另一个地区之间的气压差引起的。

 

 6）With this solar wind, there is a difference in pressure between these clouds of particles and other points in space. 

对于太阳风而言，这些粒子云和太空中的其他地方之间有压力差。

 

7）Because of that difference, solar wind travels out from the sun toward the regions of less pressure.

由于这种差异，太阳风从太阳吹向气压较小的区域。(3-B)

 

8） And although it travels more slowly than light, it travels far. 

虽然它的传播速度比光慢，但它传播得很远。

 

9）We know, for example, that the solar wind reaches beyond the orbit of the dwarf planet Pluto, in the outer regions of the solar system. (2)

例如，我们知道太阳风的传播距离超出了矮行星冥王星的轨道，太阳系的外缘区域。

 

10） In fact, research suggests solar wind could be used to propel spacecraft in much the same way we harness wind to propel boats on earth. (3-C)

事实上，研究表明太阳风可以用来推动航天器，就像我们利用风推动地球上的船只一样。

 

11）Designs have been proposed for spacecraft with sails to catch and ride the solar wind. I'll post some readings about that at the class website. 

有人提议设计带有帆的航天器来捕捉和利用太阳风。我会在班级网站上发布一些相关阅读资料。

 

12）[That sounds really cool. Does solar wind affect us in any way now? ] (1)

听起来很酷。现在太阳风对我们有什么影响吗？

 

13）Well, generally, solar wind is deflected(使转向) away by the magnetic field that surrounds earth. 

一般来说，太阳风会被地球的磁场所偏转。


14）When the charged particles of solar wind reach that magnetic field, it tends to deflect them toward either earth's north pole or its south pole.

当太阳风的带电粒子到达地球磁场时，地球磁场往往会使它们转向地球的北极或南极偏转。


15）Solar wind can enter our atmosphere at the poles, though, because the magnetic field is weaker, there. (4)

不过，由于南北极的磁场较弱，太阳风可以从两极进入大气层。

 

16）[And that creates the northern lights, right?]

这就产生了北极光，对吗？

 

17）Yes. It does. All those solar wind particles near the poles, they collide with(碰撞) gaseous particles in earths atmosphere, creating light that we can see in the night sky.    

对，确实如此。所有靠近两极的太阳风粒子都会和地球大气中的气态粒子碰撞 ，产生我们可以在夜空中看到的光。

 

18）The color of the light varies with the type of gas involved. 

光的颜色会因为气体的类型而不同。

 

19）We call this phenomenon the northern lights when it occurs near earth’s North Pole, and the southern lights, when it occurs is near the South Pole.

当这种现象发生在地球北极附近时，我们称之为北极光，当它发生在南极附近时，我们称之为南极光。（就可劲儿水时长）

 

 20）In the past, it was believed that the poles were the only place where solar wind enters our own atmosphere or at least that it was very rare for solar wind to enter at other points. 

过去，人们认为两极是太阳风进入我们大气层的唯一地方，或者至少太阳风在其他地方进入大气层是非常罕见的。

 

21）But new evidence suggests its more common than we thought, and that can have a lot of effects, especially when the sun spots occur. 

但新的证据表明，它比我们想象的更为普遍，并且可能产生很多影响，尤其是当太阳黑子出现时。

 

22) Do you remember what sun spots are? 

还记得什么是太阳黑子吗？

 

23）Magnetic storms on the surface of the sun. 

太阳表面的磁暴。


24）Right, and those storms create additional charged particles that solar wind can carry toward earth.

没错，这些风暴产生了额外的带电粒子，太阳风可以把这些粒子带到地球上。

 

25 )[Any ideas what impact this added influx(大量涌入) of particles can have?]

这些大量涌入的带电粒子会产生什么影响呢？有想法吗？

 

26)  It can disrupt radio broadcasts and satellite transmissions. 

它可以干扰无线电广播和卫星传输。

 

27) It can also have an effect on electric power grids.

它还可能对电网产生影响。

 

28) Right. Charged particles from the sun entering our atmosphere can have a big impact on communications and power systems. 

没错，来自太阳的带电粒子进入大气层会对通信和电力系统产生巨大影响。

 

29) That should be happening all the time, though. If solar wind in the atmosphere is common. (6)

不过，这种影响不应该一直在发生吗？如果大气中的太阳风很常见的话。

 

30) It's a question of degree, we do get protection from earth's magnetic field, so when there are no magnetic storms on the sun, the effects are minimal. 

这是一个程度的问题，我们确实还是受到了地球磁场的保护，所以当太阳上没有磁暴时，（太阳风对我们的）影响是很小的。

 

31) Instead of acting as a barrier(屏障), as we thought, the magnetic field acts more like a sieve(筛子).

与其说是充当屏障, 磁场的作用更像一个筛子。


32 )It lets small amounts of solar winds seep through in some locations other than the poles. The results aren't catastrophic, though.

它可以让少量的太阳风从两极以外的一些地方渗入大气层。不过结果并不是灾难性的。


33) [Now, solar wind can also affect our climate and weather.] 

太阳风也会影响我们的气候和天气。

 

34) We've seen solar phenomena that coincide with weather phenomena on earth.

我们已经看到太阳上的某种现象与地球上的天气现象恰好同时发生。

 

35) For example, sunspots occur in cycles, and a period when sunspot activity is at its lowest, when there is less solar wind, that period is known as a solar minimum.

例如，太阳黑子以周期的形式出现，当太阳黑子活动处于最低水平时，即当太阳风较少时，这一时期被称为太阳活动极小期。


36 ) Research indicates that many solar minimum events have coincided with drought in some areas on Earth. 

研究表明，在地球上的一些地区，许多太阳活动极小期正好与地球上的干旱同时发生。

 

37) Do the changes in solar wind play a role in that? We don't know for sure. 

太阳风的变化在其中起作用吗？我们不能肯定。


38) If we found out, could we do anything to prevent droughts?

如果我们弄清楚了两者的联系，我们能做些什么来防止干旱吗？

 

39) We still couldn't avoid the drought itself, but perhaps just paying attention to cycles of sun spot activity would allow us to better prepare for those drought periods by conserving water in advance, for example. (5)

我们仍然无法避免干旱本身，但也许仅仅关注太阳黑子活动的周期就可以让我们通过提前储存水资源，以更好地为这些干旱期做好准备。

Listen to part of a lecture in an astronomy class. 

 

So, we pretty much know how the biggest stars die. When a massive star reaches the end of its existence, it explodes sending out a huge blast of energy which then fades to invisibility within a few weeks or months. When a giant star explodes like this, it's called a supernova. To the naked eye, a supernova appears to be a bright new star, but it's not a new star. It's a dying star. However, the shock waves from a supernova excite nearby clouds of hydrogen gas causing them to compress which does form new stars. 


我们大概知道最大的恒星的死亡过程。当一颗大质量恒星走到终点时，它会爆炸，散发出巨大的能量，这些能量会在几周或几个月内消失不见。当一颗巨星像这样爆炸时，它被称为超新星。在肉眼看来，超新星似乎是一颗明亮的新星，但它不是一颗新星。这是一颗正在死亡的恒星。然而，来自超新星的冲击波会激发附近的氢气云，使其压缩，从而形成新恒星。

 

And that process emits a distinctive type of radiation. Now, a particular interest to us today is that the same type of radiation has been observed in places where galaxies have collided.


这个过程会发出一种独特的辐射。现在，我们特别感兴趣的是，在星系碰撞的地方也观察到了同样类型的辐射。

 

In those same areas, we've also observed a high rate of new star formation. So not surprisingly, we've had a theory that colliding galaxies caused the new star formation. But there was no direct evidence, that is until recently, when some European astronomers found clouds of excited hydrogen gas that could only have been caused by shock waves from a pair of colliding galaxies. (2) You know we used to think of galaxies as isolated systems, like islands in the sea of space. Now we know that they are always moving usually in clusters. I mean, think about all the gravitational forces involved. We're talking about huge systems of stars and gas and dust. And since the average separation between galaxies is only about 20 times their diameter, it isn't surprising that they often meet. We call it an encounter and they meet in a variety of ways.①


在这些地区，我们还观察到了高比率的新星形成。因此，我们有了一个理论，即星系碰撞导致了新恒星的形成。但直到最近，我们才找到这个理论的直接证据。一些欧洲天文学家发现被激发的氢气云可能是由一对碰撞星系的冲击波引起的。你知道，我们过去认为星系是孤立的系统，就像太空海洋中的岛屿。现在我们知道它们通常是成群移动的。考虑下所有涉及的引力。星系可是由恒星、气体和尘埃组成的巨大系统。（强调引力大）由于星系之间的平均间隔只有其直径的20倍左右，因此它们经常相遇也就不足为奇了。我们称之为“相遇”，他们“相遇”的方式有很多种。


Mergers, for example, they are the most extreme kinds of Galaxy encounters.③-1 A merger is when two galaxies meet and they don't have enough momentum to keep going on their separate ways, so they merge becoming one galaxy. Usually a larger galaxy will absorb sort of swallow a smaller one. This can trigger huge areas of star formation as enormous clouds of gas from the two galaxies collide and collapse into very active star forming regions. 


例如，合并是星系相遇中最极端的一种。合并是指两个星系相遇时，它们没有足够的动量分开了，所以它们合并成为一个星系。通常一个较大的星系会吞并一个较小的星系。当来自两个星系的巨大气体云碰撞并坍缩成非常活跃的恒星形成区域时，这可能会触发巨大的恒星形成区域。


Collisions are less violent than mergers.③-2 That is they create fewer or relatively less active areas of star formation. The galaxies pass through each other, each going it separate way after they collide. The collision sends out a ripple of energy into space, plowing gas and dust in front of it. Your textbook has a great picture of a Galaxy collision taken by the Hubble space telescope. See the Cartwheel Galaxy on the left. It looks like the ripples in a pond after his stones been tossed in. That's because one of the small galaxies on the rights pass through it, and that bright ring around it, that's where colliding and compressing gas is forming stars.


碰撞没有合并那么剧烈。也就是说，它们产生的恒星形成区域较少或相对较不活跃。星系相互穿过，碰撞后各自分开。碰撞向太空发出能量涟漪，将气体和灰尘扫向前方。你的教科书中有一张哈勃太空望远镜拍摄的星系碰撞的照片。看到左边的车轮星系。看起来像是他的石头被扔进去后池塘里的涟漪。这是因为右边的一个小星系穿过它，而它周围的明亮光环，就是碰撞和压缩气体形成恒星的地方。


But which Galaxy collided with it? The one on the upper right has been stripped of gas and dust, which is one clear sign that there has been a collision. But the one on the bottom showing a lot of new star formation, which is also evidence of a collision. We are just not sure. (4)


但哪个星系与它发生了碰撞？右上角的星系有气体和灰尘被剥离出来，这是发生碰撞的一个明显迹象。但底部的星系显示了许多新恒星的形成，这也是碰撞的证据。我们不确定（到底车轮星系和哪个星系发生了碰撞）。

 

Finally, we have interactions. In this type of encounter, the galaxies exchange gas and dust, but don't actually collide. (3)  Many astronomers believe that the trail of gas and dust that stretches from our milky way Galaxy to one nearby is the result of an interaction. When the gravitational forces of the Milky Way pull some dust and gas off of the other Galaxy. (5)

 

最后一种相遇方式是“互动”。在这种相遇方式中，星系交换气体和尘埃，但实际上并不碰撞。 许多天文学家认为，从我们的银河系延伸到附近银河系的气体和尘埃轨迹是“互动”的结果。当银河系的引力将一些尘埃和气体从另一个星系拉走时。


Student: What about stars? Do they ever collide? 

Professor: Actually almost never. Stars are very small compared to the size of a Galaxy and they're pretty far apart from each other. Small stars, that term's relative, isn't it? It's hard to imagine the massive distances we are talking about here. Even encounter seems like an odd term as these events take a long time. We're talking billions of years, which is why most of what we theorize about comes from computer simulations. Otherwise, all we'd see is a sort of freeze frame of whatever stage the encounter is in a single picture out of a long sequence of events. But I mentioned some new evidence that's come from a pair of galaxies called the Antennae. The Antennae are at an early stage of encounter, but will probably be a merger and excited hydrogen gas has been found in the regions where the two galaxies overlap. We know because that distinctive radiation signature is present, and we know that there aren't enough supernovae to explain the radiation. The only other possibility is that we're seeing the result of shock waves produced by the collision of these two galaxies.(6)

 

学生：那恒星呢？它们曾经碰撞过吗？（偏离文章主题的问题）

教授：几乎没有。与星系大小相比，恒星非常小，而且彼此相距甚远。小是相对于星系而言。（您刚说过了）很难想象我们在这里谈论的是多么遥远的距离。“相遇”似乎是一个奇怪的术语，因为这些事件需要很长时间。我们谈论的是数十亿年，这就是为什么我们的理论大部分来自计算机模拟。否则，我们所看到的只是一种定格的画面（真·静止画面），在一系列事件当中的一个瞬间的照片。但我提到了一些新的证据，来自一对叫做A的星系。A处于“相遇”的早期阶段，但可能是一次合并（最激烈的那种碰撞），在两个星系重叠的区域发现了被激发的氢气。我们之所以知道，是因为存在着独特的辐射特征，而且我们知道没有足够的超新星来解释这种关系。（排除一个错误选项）唯一的另一种可能性是我们看到了这两个星系碰撞产生的冲击波的结果。
