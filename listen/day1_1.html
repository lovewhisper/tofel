Listen to part of a lecture in a biology class. 

 

1) When we hear the word bacteria, a lot of people think germs or disease, but bacteria can be good or bad and if it weren’t for the good bacteria, we wouldn’t be able to survive. (1)

当我们听到细菌这个词时，很多人都会想到病菌或疾病，但细菌有好有坏，如果没有好的细菌，我们就无法生存。

2) But before getting into that let me reiterate that bacteria are virtually everywhere.

但在讨论这个问题之前，让我重申，细菌几乎无处不在。

3) There are bacteria living in soil, living on windowsills, they’re living on us, and they’re living in us.

有细菌生活在土壤中，生活在窗台上，它们生活在我们身上，而且它们生活在我们体内。

4) As you know, bacteria are organisms; tiny, single-celled organisms.

如你所知，细菌是生物；微小的单细胞生物。

5) They move, they breathe, they do a lot of the things that we do.

它们移动，它们呼吸，它们做很多我们做的事情。（麻雀虽小五脏俱全属于是）

6) They have metabolic activities.

它们有新陈代谢活动。

7) They can break down sugars, build proteins, and secrete things and all these things happen just on a much smaller and faster scale.

它们可以分解糖类，构建蛋白质，并分泌物质，这一切都是以更小的规模和更快的速度发生。

8) A number of research studies have shown and now, this is hard to believe, but the number of bacterial cells in and on our body actually exceeds the number of our human cells. 

一些研究表明，这很难以置信，但我们体内和身上的细菌细胞的数量实际上超过了我们人类细胞的数量。

9) If you consider all the human and bacterial cells that exist in and on our body, only one in every ten cells in our body is human. 

如果计算一下我们体内外所有人类和细菌细胞的数量，会发现每十个细胞中只有一个是人类的细胞。

10) The rest are mostly bacteria. 

其余的大部分是细菌。

11) Now, because the bacterial cells are much smaller, that doesn’t mean that we are 90% bacteria, but still, I find that amazing. 

当然，因为细菌细胞要小得多，这并不意味着我们90%（的物质）是细菌，但我仍然觉得这很惊人。

12) Nine of ten cells are bacteria. (2) 

十个细胞中有九个是细菌。

13) So, why do we have all these bacteria?

那么，为什么我们有这么多的细菌？

14) At least 500 different species in the digestive system alone.

仅在消化系统中就至少有500个不同的物种。

15) And why do I say they’re helpful?

为什么我说它们是有益的呢？

16) Let’s look at some examples.

让我们来看一些例子。

17) First, our bodies can’t make certain vitamins, like certain B-vitamins and vitamin-K, which plays an important role in blood clotting. 

首先，我们的身体不能制造某些维生素，如某些B族维生素和维生素K，维生素K在血液凝固中起重要作用。

18) So, we have these good bacteria in our digestive system. 

因此，我们的消化系统中有这些好细菌。

19) They eat the food we eat and secrete those vitamins for us. (3-C)

它们吃我们吃的食物，为我们分泌这些维生素。

20) Also, some bacteria help us digest food that our own digestive enzymes cannot otherwise process.

此外，一些细菌还帮助我们消化我们自己的消化酶无法处理的食物。

21) For example, at least one bacterial species breaks down the complex carbohydrates that are found in vegetables and other plant foods. 

例如，至少有一种细菌可以分解蔬菜和其他植物食物中的复合碳水化合物。

22) Our own enzymes can’t do this for us. (3-A)

我们体内的酶消化不了这些物质。

23) The bacteria in our digestive system also compete for the same nutrients that bad bacteria need. (3-E) You know, the ones that if allowed to grow unchecked can be harmful to us. 

我们消化系统中的好细菌也争夺坏细菌所需要的营养物质。坏细菌指的哪些如果放任不管就会对我们有害的细菌。（废话文学十级）

24) There’re so many more good bacteria than bad bacteria normally that the good ones overwhelm the bad ones by taking up most of the available nutrients.

好的细菌比坏的细菌多得多，它们通过占用大部分可用的营养物质去碾压坏细菌。

25) The bacteria on our skin do a similar thing. 

我们皮肤上的细菌也有类似的作用。

26) They prevent the bad bacteria from getting a foothold, so they really serve as another layer of protection against infection.

它们防止坏细菌立足，可以说它们相当于预防感染的保护层。

27) So, when you wash your hands with antibacterial soap, you know, the soap that helps kill bacteria when you use it, well, what does that do?

所以当你用抗菌肥皂洗手时，也就是哪些能杀死细菌的肥皂，会发生什么呢？

28) It’s going to kill the bacteria and not just the bad bacteria. 

洗手会杀死细菌，而且不仅仅是坏细菌。

29) The soap doesn’t discriminate between the good and bad. 

肥皂不分好坏。

30) A lot of the good bacteria that’s protecting you will be destroyed as well. (4)

很多好细菌也会被杀死。

31) Another thing that inadvertently kills good bacteria are antibiotics. 

另一个无意中杀死好细菌的东西是抗生素。（二位要拜把子吗？）

32) Most of them are what we call broad-spectrum antibiotics, which means they kill all kinds of bacteria, not just the bad ones.

它们中的大多数都是我们所说的广谱抗生素，这意味着它们会杀死所有种类的细菌，而不仅仅是坏细菌。（没想到这个程度的废话文学还有进步的空间。。。）

33) So, to replenish some of the good bacteria, we can take probiotics like the ones contained in yogurt. 

因此，为了补充一些好细菌，我们可以服用益生菌，如酸奶中含有的益生菌。（这真的不是插播广告？）

34) There’s a bacterium used to help make yogurt and it’s the same kind of bacterium that can aid digestion, so if you get sick and you’re given antibiotics the doctor might tell you to eat yogurt. (6-A)

有一种细菌用来帮助制造酸奶，它也是可以帮助消化的那种细菌，所以如果你生病了，在服用抗生素，医生可能会告诉你吃酸奶。

35) That’s one form of probiotic therapy. 

这是益生菌疗法的一种形式。

36) The doctor would actually say to you to eat yogurt with live cultures, with live bacteria. 

医生实际上会对你说吃有活菌的酸奶。

37) You see, all yogurt is made with live bacteria, but some yogurt brands destroy the bacteria in the processing. (5)

虽然所有的酸奶都是用活细菌制成的，但有些酸奶品牌在加工过程中破坏了细菌。

38) Anyway, there’s plenty of evidence that probiotics can help treat or prevent certain digestive problems,(6-A) but if you go into a health food store you may see probiotic supplements claiming to lower blood pressure and do things beyond improving gastrointestinal health.

总之，有很多证据表明，益生菌可以帮助治疗或预防某些消化道问题，但如果你进入一家健康食品商店，你可能会看到益生菌补充剂声称可以降低血压，并做一些改善胃肠道健康以外的事情。

39) While these claims may very well be documented in the future, right now they’re merely postulations based on laboratory data, test tube experiments, not on what happens when people actually take probiotics. (6-D)

虽然这些声称的药效会在未来被证实，但是现在它们只是基于实验室数据和实验，并不是基于人们实际服用益生菌时的情况。

Listen to part of a lecture in a biology class. 


When we hear the word bacteria, a lot of people think germs or disease, but bacteria can be good or bad and if it weren’t for the good bacteria, we wouldn’t be able to survive. (1)


But before getting into that let me reiterate that bacteria are virtually everywhere. There are bacteria living in soil, living on windowsills, they’re living on us, and they’re living in us. As you know, bacteria are organisms; tiny, single celled organisms. They move, they breathe, they do a lot of the things that we do. They have metabolic activities. They can break down sugars, build proteins, and secrete things and all these things happen just on a much smaller and faster scale.


A number of research studies have shown and now, this is hard to believe, but the number of bacterial cells in and on our body actually exceeds the number of our human cells. If you consider all the human and bacterial cells that exist in and on our body, only one in every ten cells in our body is human. The rest are mostly bacteria. Now, because the bacteria cells are much smaller, that doesn’t mean that we are 90% bacteria, but still, I find that amazing. Nine of ten cells are bacteria. (2) So, why do we have all these bacteria? At least 500 different species in the digestive system alone and why do I say they’re helpful? Let’s look at some examples.


First, our bodies can’t make certain vitamins, like certain B-vitamins and vitamin-K, which plays an important role in blood clotting. So, we have these good bacteria in our digestive system. They eat the food we eat and secrete those vitamins for us. (3-C)


Also, some bacteria help us digest food that our own digestive enzymes cannot otherwise process. For example, at least one bacterial species breaks down the complex carbohydrates that are found in vegetables and other plant foods. Our own enzymes can’t do this for us. (3-A) The bacteria in our digestive system also compete for the same nutrients that bad bacteria need. (3-E) You know, the ones that if allowed to grow unchecked can be harmful to us. There’re so many more good bacteria than bad bacteria normally that the good ones overwhelm the bad ones by taking up most of the available nutrients.


The bacteria on our skin do a similar thing. They prevent the bad bacteria from getting a foothold, so they really serve as another layer of protection against infection, so when you wash your hands with antibacterial soap, you know, the soap that helps kill bacteria when you use it, well, what does that do? It’s going to kill the bacteria and not just the bad bacteria. The soap doesn’t discriminate between the good and bad. A lot of the good bacteria that’s protecting you will be destroyed as well. (4)



Another thing that inadvertently kills good bacteria are antibiotics. Most of them are what we call broad-spectrum antibiotics, which means they kill all kinds of bacteria, not just the bad ones. So, to replenish some of the good bacteria, we can take probiotics, like the ones contained in yogurt. There’s a bacterium used to help make yogurt and it’s the same kind of bacterium that can aid digestion, so if you get sick and you’re given antibiotics the doctor might tell you to eat yogurt. (6-A) That’s one form of probiotic therapy. The doctor would actually say to you to eat yogurt with live cultures, with live bacteria. You see, all yogurt is made with live bacteria, but some yogurt brands destroy the bacteria in the processing. (5)


Anyway, there’s plenty of evidence that probiotics can help treat or prevent certain digestive problems,(6-A)  but if you go into a health food store you may see probiotic supplements claiming to lower blood pressure and do things beyond improving gastrointestinal health. While these claims may very well be documented in the future, right now they’re merely postulations based on laboratory data, test tube experiments, not on what happens when people actually take probiotics. (6-D)
