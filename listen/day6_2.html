6. What does the professor imply about the equilibrium theory when she discusses events on Krakatoa? 


A. The theory no longer worked for Krakatoa after the volcanic eruption there

B. Events on Krakatoa are consistent with the theory

C. The theory explains the immigration rate on Krakatoa, but not the extinction rate

D. Events on Krakatoa provide evidence that contradicts the theory



























答案：

C BC AD D DACB B





文本：


Listen to part of a lecture in an ecology class. 


So we're talking about biogeography. Biogeography is the geographic distribution of plants and animals in an environment. As you know, biogeography varies from ecosystem to ecosystem. 

所以我们讨论过了生物地理学。生物地理学是指植物和动物在环境中的地理分布。正如你们所知，这种分布因生态系统而异。


Now, one type of ecosystem that we haven't discussed yet is the island environment. Now islands have always been interesting to study because their isolation makes them... it creates a kind of laboratory for examining their ecosystems.

现在，我们还没有讨论过的一种生态系统是岛屿环境。岛屿一直是研究的热点，因为它们的与世隔绝使它们。。。构成了一种便于我们研究它的生态系统的实验室环境。


In the 1960s, Robert MacArthur E.O.Wilson developed a theory, they called the equilibrium theory of island biogeography. And, their theory states that over time islands achieve equilibrium, a balance in terms of the number of species of plants, animals, insects, etc, that they can support. 

20世纪60年代，RM提出了一种理论，他们称之为岛屿生物地理学的平衡理论。而且，他们的理论表明，随着时间的推移，（环境）会达到平衡（态），即在植物、动物、昆虫等物种的数量方面达到平衡，而这些物种是他们能够支持的。


And, they, that is MacArthur and Wilson, developed calculations to predict the point at which equilibrium is achieved and the number of species that a given island would be able to sustain. 

他们，即MA和W，进行了计算，以预测达到平衡的点以及某个岛屿能够维持的物种数量。


According to their theory, this number, the total number of species depends on two things, the size and location of the island. (2)For example, they predict that there will be more species on bigger islands and that there will be more species on islands that are closer to the mainland. 

根据他们的理论，这个数量，物种的总数取决于两件事，岛的大小和位置。例如，他们预测在更大的岛屿上会有更多的物种，以及在离大陆更近的岛屿上会有更多的物种。


So how does this work? According to MacArthur and Wilson, there are two opposing forces constantly at work in an island environment, pushing the island toward the state of equilibrium. 

这两个因素具体是如何起作用的呢？根据MA和W的说法，在岛屿环境中，有两股对立的力量不断在起作用，将岛屿推向平衡状态。


The first force is the immigration rate. The immigration rate tracks the addition of new species to an island. And they argue that this rate declines over time that there are more new species early on when the islands still empty and there's a little competition for space. However, as time progresses, there will be fewer and fewer new species that can compete with established species. 

第一个因素是迁入率。迁入率对应一个岛屿新物种的增加。他们认为，随着时间的推移，这一比率会下降，因为在岛上还空着的时候，早期就有更多的新物种，而且对空间也有一些竞争。然而，随着时间的推移，能够与现有物种竞争的新物种将越来越少。


It's not impossible for new species to be introduced at this late stage. It might adapt better to some it changed in the evolving habitat, but it is definitely tougher. 

在这个后期阶段引入新物种并非不可能。它可能会更好地适应在不断进化的栖息地中发生的一些变化，但它肯定更难适应。


The second force is the extinction rate. And this is the rate at which species disappear from the island. In contrast with the immigration rate, this is a force that increases over time. In the beginning when there's plenty of space species don't need to compete with each other quite so much.

第二个因素是灭绝率。这是物种从岛上消失的速度。与迁入率相比，这是一种随时间而增加的力量。在一开始，当有大量的太空物种时，它们不需要如此激烈地相互竞争。


But as species get settled on the island and the amount of space for each species declines conditions become much more competitive. And it's not just the weakest species that may disappear. On a crowded island, there are likely to be smaller numbers of every species which makes them all more vulnerable to things like it was sudden changes in the climate or or some kind of natural disaster. (3)

但随着物种在岛上定居，每个物种的生存空间减少，竞争日渐激烈。可能消失的不仅仅是最弱的物种。在一个拥挤的岛屿上，每个物种的数量可能都会减少，这使得它们更容易受到气候突变或某种自然灾害的影响。



So there are these two opposing forces and at a certain point in the island's ecological evolution, they cancel each other out. Immigration matches extinction. At that point, equilibrium is achieved. If you accept these assumptions, then MacArthur and Wilson predictions about size and location makes sense. 

所以有这两种对立的力量，在生态进化的某一点上，它们相互抵消。移民与灭绝相匹配。在这一点上，平衡就实现了。如果你接受这些假设，那么MA和W关于大小和位置的预测是合理的。


If an island is bigger, then there's more space for a greater number of species to compete and survive, if one island is closer or easier to get to from the mainland than another, then more species will be able to immigrate to the island from the mainland and establish themselves there before... before a state of equilibrium occurs. 

如果一个岛屿更大，那么就有更多的空间让更多的物种竞争和生存，如果一个岛屿比另一个岛屿更接近或更容易从大陆到达，那么更多的物种将能够从大陆“移民”到该岛，并在平衡状态出现之前安定下来。


Now notice what this theory doesn't predict. It doesn't say a thing about what type of species will be on the island. It doesn't predict which new species will arrive or which existing species will disappear. (4)

现在注意这个理论没有预测到什么。它没有说明岛上会有什么种类的物种。它不能预测哪些新物种将到来，哪些现有物种将消失。


However, we can get some sense for what those species might be, by looking at the example of Krakatoa. You've probably heard of Krakatoa, an island in Indonesia. It has a volcano that erupted in 1883. The destruction of the ecosystem was total. Nothing survived on that island. It only took a few years before the islands started to support life again, but it couldn't just recreate its old ecosystem. 

然而，通过观察K岛的例子，我们可以了解这些物种可能是什么。你可能听说过印尼的K岛。它有一座1883年喷发的火山。生态系统遭到彻底破坏。那个岛上什么也没留下。仅仅过了几年，这些岛屿才开始重新出现了生命，但它不可能重建之前的生态系统了。


The first species to take hold were some weeds, the sort of pioneering organisms that do well after the collapse of an ecosystem. Beatles and spiders also returned. In time a a grassland ecosystem developed. 

最早出现的物种是一些杂草，这是一种在生态系统崩溃后表现良好的先锋生物。甲壳虫和蜘蛛也回来了。随着时间的推移，草原生态系统得到了发展。


So once there was some more established plant life, birds and bats were attracted to the island. They in turn brought... dropped seeds from fruit bearing trees. As trees gained foothold on the island, forests eventually replaced the grasslands. (5) And some pioneer species disappeared. The ones that couldn't survive in a forest environment. 

因此，一旦有了更成熟的植物，鸟类和蝙蝠就被吸引到岛上。他们又带来了果树的种子。随着树木在岛上站稳脚跟，森林最终取代了草地。一些先锋物种消失了。那些无法在森林环境中生存的动物。


But does Krakatoa approve the equilibrium theory? Well, nothing about it disproves it so far. (6) Immigration and extinction rates have generally behaved according to the theory. 

The number of species on the island corresponds with the authors' calculations and seems to be stable. It appears that the island has achieved the type of equilibrium state that the theory predicts. However, some recent studies suggest that other factors may also play roles in the development of biogeography. 

但是，K岛的例子证明了均衡理论吗？到目前为止，它至少没有提供反面的证据。迁入率和灭绝率通常和理论的预测相符。

岛上物种的数量与作者的计算一致，似乎是稳定的。看来，该岛已经达到了理论预测的那种平衡状态。然而，最近的一些研究表明，其他因素也可能在动植物的地理分布中发挥作用。
