1）So when we talk about European painters and styles of the 1800s, it's important to remember that when they first arrived on the scene, they were quite unpopular with the academies. 

因此，当我们谈论19世纪的欧洲画家和风格时，重要的是要记住，当他们首次登上历史舞台的时候，他们非常不受学院派的欢迎。

 

 2）The academies were the established art societies affiliated with(与。。。相关) prestigious galleries and schools for young artists. 

学院派是与著名的画廊和培养年轻艺术家的学校有深入联系的艺术圈。

 

3）They were usually sponsored by the state.

这些画廊和学校通常由国家赞助。

 

4）This was true for a group of painters in France who were to be called impressionists. (1)

法国的一群被称为印象派的画家就是不受欢迎的新兴画派之一。

 

5）The impressionists, although quite popular today, did not have the approval of these academies, so it was difficult for them to get their work shown. 

印象派画家虽然在今天很受欢迎，但当时没有得到学院派的认可，因此，他们很难展示自己的作品。

 

6）[In France, the Academy was quite powerful in the 1800s,]it actually determined what type of paintings were painted and sold. 

在法国，艺术学院在19世纪非常有权利，它实际上决定了绘画和销售的类型。

 

7）Since it ran schools and galleries, it also had a monopoly(垄断权) on public taste(品味). 

因为学院经营学校和画廊，所以它也拥有对大众品味的垄断权.

 

8）There were annual exhibitions where artists could display their work and get noticed, and that was important. 

学院每年都举办展览，艺术家们可以在那里展示他们的作品并引起注意，这一点至关重要。


9）Young artist whose work was accepted at an academy exhibition might become quite well known. 

作品在学院展览上被接受的年轻艺术家可能会一夜成名。

 

10）And showing one's work there could lead to prestigious prizes and well-paying commissions(委托). 

在那里展示自己的作品可以获得代表着很高荣誉的奖项和报酬丰厚的委托.（俗称订单）

 

11）It was the path to success for artists at that time. (2)

这就是当时艺术家取得成功的必经之路。

 

12）So an artist's career basically depended on showing their work at one of these exhibitions？

艺术家的职业生涯基本上取决于在其中一个展览上展示他们的作品？

 

13）Very much so. What's more the Academy dictated the content of the paintings they'd accept and the techniques artists could use. 

基本上就是这样。此外，学院还规定了他们会接受的绘画内容和艺术家可以使用的技巧。

 

14）The academy was pretty conservative(保守的), as far as colors, technique, for example, no visible brush strokes(笔触). They wanted a highly polished look with no evidence of how the paintbrush had moved to create it. 

学院很保守, 在颜色、技术的使用而言都是如此，比如作品不能有可见的笔触。 他们想要一个高度光滑的外观，不留画笔创作的痕迹。

 

 15）To the Academy, the impressionists' dabs and streaks of paint looked unfinished(未完成的), as if the work still needed another finer layer of paint.

对学院来说，印象派画家的蘸痕和条痕看起来是未完成的状态, 好像这件作品还需要再加一个更加细腻的涂层。

 

16）And well, you read about the Impressionist for homework. [You tell me, why weren't they accepted by the academy? ]

你应该读了关于印象派的一些内容，作业里布置了。你们来告诉我，为什么印象派画家没有被学院接受？

 

17）Well, they used bright colors for one, and their, uh, their brush strokes were definitely visible. (3-C)

他们使用了明亮的颜色，而且他们的笔触清晰可见。

 

18）Good, what else? 

很好，还有什么？

 

19）Subject matter. I mean, you didn't see them painting any of those historical subjects we talked about last week, like famous battles, kings and queens, characters from mythology(神话). 

还有主题。他们没画我们上周谈到的任何历史题材，比如著名的战役、国王和王后、神话中的人物。（可以忽略的列举）

 

20）Right. They painted landscapes, informal portraits, portraits of everyday people, workers, their own family subjects that the Academy would consider unimportant. 

对，他们画风景画、非正式肖像画、普通人肖像画、工人肖像画以及学院认为不重要的家庭题材。（平凡人，平凡事）

 

21）And technique, the academy wanted somber(昏暗的) tones, not the bright blues and yellows that covered the impressionist’s canvases(帆布). 

还有技术方面，学院想要昏暗的色调，而不是印象派画布上明亮的蓝色和黄色

 

22）So. It's not surprising that many gifted impressionists were rejected by academy juries. 

所以许多有天赋的印象派画家被学院陪审团拒绝，这并不奇怪。


23）[So what did they do?] I mean, they did eventually become well known, so how did their work get out there? 

他们做了什么？我是说，他们最终还是成名了，那么他们是如何让自己的作品变得广为人知的呢？

 

24）Well, together they organize group shows of their own. 

他们一起组织自己的团体展览。

 

25）But even before the Impressionist did so, other artists who had been rejected by the academy had begun exhibiting in their private studios(画室), inviting the public. 

但即使印象派画家自行举办展览之前，其他被学院拒绝的艺术家已经开始在他们的私人工作室开始展出作品了, 并且邀请了公众来参观。

 

26）Courbet, who painted in a style called realism, went one step further,

C以一种叫做写实主义的风格作画，他更进一步，

 

27）and open his own private exhibition area at the universal Exhibition in Paris in 1855. (4)

于1855年在巴黎世界博览会上开设了自己的私人展览区。

 

28）[So what happened, did people applaud him, for, you know, for courage? ]

当时的反响怎么样？人们为他的勇气喝彩了吗？

 

29）On the contrary, they thought it was conceited of him. 

正好，他们认为他很自负。

 

30）Such self promotion and from an artist, only merchants could indulge in such common behavior. (6)

他们认为这是令人不齿自我推销，而且还来自于艺术家，只有商人才能做出这种俗不可耐的行径。

 

31）But Courbet did it again some years later, and another French painter, Manet, joined him setting up his own exhibition. 

但几年后C又举办了自己的展览，另一位法国画家马奈也加入了他的行列，创办了自己的展览。（这位是印象派的掌门人）

 

32）Manet also persuaded art dealers to give him one person shows, which was also unheard of. 

马奈还说服艺术品经销商为他举办个展，这也是前所未闻的。


33）But this brought the artist some success. 

但这给这位艺术家带来了一些成功。

 

34）So the impressionist, following the lead of these other artists, held several group exhibitions between 1874 and 1886. 

因此这位印象派画家在其他艺术家的带领下，于1874年至1886年间举办了几次集体展览。

 

35）[Now,] what united this group even more so than style was their desire to gain independence from the academy. 

将这个群体团结在一起不仅仅是他们的绘画风格，更重要的是他们从学院获得独立的渴望。

 

36）Since it turned out that they didn't need to depend on the academy for exhibit space. 

因为事实证明，他们不需要依靠学院来获得展览空间。

 

37）They could continue flagrantly breaking all the rules, and their work might gain acceptance on its own. (5) 

他们可以继续公然违反所有规则，他们的作品可以独立获得认可。

 

38）And in fact, art dealers in Paris bought, sold and promoted impressionist paintings.

事实上，巴黎的艺术经销商买卖并推广了印象派绘画。

 

39） And eventually, by the late nineteenth century, the general public all around the world began to embrace(接受) them. 

最终，到了十九世纪末，全世界的公众开始接受他们

 

40）American collectors were largely responsible for this reversal of fortunes, since they were mainly the ones who bought enough paintings to keep several artists at work. 

美国收藏家在很大程度上是造成这一局面，因为他们是大主顾。

 

41）The first exhibition of impressionist art in an actual museum occurred in 1897.

1897年，印象派艺术首次在一家真正的博物馆展出。

 

 42）And an exhibition at the 1900 World Exposition sealed the artists reputations and worldwide popularity. 

1900年世界博览会上的一场展览为艺术家们赢得了声誉和世界知名度。

 

43）The effect was not only commercial but artistic, too, because as I mentioned, there were no style constraints for these independent exhibitions. 

其效果不仅是商业性的，而且是艺术性的，因为正如我所提到的，这些独立展览没有风格限制。

 

44）Since artists could now explore new forms of expression, they didn't feel pressure to paint a certain subject or in a certain way to get the academy's approval(许可). A wide variety of styles developed. (5)

由于艺术家们现在可以探索新的表达形式，他们没有感到需要画某个主题或以某种方式获得学院批准的压力，多种风格得到了发展。
