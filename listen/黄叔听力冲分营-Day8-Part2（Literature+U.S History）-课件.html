Listen to part of a lecture in a United States history class. 


I'd like to talk today about an important paradigm in the history of the United States and how it came to be changed upon a second look.(1) 

今天，我想谈谈美国历史上的一个重要的典型范例，以及它是如何在我们重新审视后发生改变的。


In 1893, an eminent American historian by the name of Frederick Jackson Turner delivered a lecture at the American historical association. And his lecture was entitled The Significance of the Frontier in American history. Now, when he was talking about the frontier, he was referring to the part of the American west that wasn't yet settled.

1893年，美国著名历史学家FJ在美国历史协会发表演讲。他的演讲题目是《边境在美国历史上的重要性》。现在，当他谈到边境时，他指的是美国西部尚未定居的地区。（西部开发的前沿地带）


The point turner made in his lecture was that the 1890 US census had provided new data about settlement. Those data showed show that well, there was no longer a frontier. (2) This disappearance of the frontier seemed to him tremendously significant, and prompted him to gather ideas about how the frontier might have shaped American history and the American character. 

T在演讲中提出的观点是，1890年的美国人口普查提供了有关定居点的新数据。这些数据表明“前沿”已不复存在。在他看来，“前沿”的消失意义重大，这促使他开始思考“前沿”是如何塑造美国历史和美国人典型性格的。


Turner asserted that the existence of a frontier with cheaper or virtually free land for the taking, available for individuals to sort of try their luck, tended to promote certain ideals (5-B)that were central to the American character, ideals of economic self sufficiency, individualism, political independence, small town democracy, suspicion of large government, things like that. 

T断言，一个有更廉价或几乎免费的土地可供征用的，可以给人们一个试试运气的机会的“前沿”的存在，会促进某些对美国人典型性格至关重要的理想的产生，如经济自给自足、个人主义、政治独立、小镇民主、对大政府的怀疑态度等等。


He saw these things being really shaped by the economic and social conditions of the frontier. And for turner, the end of the frontier in 1890 meant the end of this particular era of American character. And that Americans would become less individualistic, less independent minded. 

他认为这些实际上是由“前沿”的经济和社会条件决定的。对T而言，1890年“前沿”的消失意味着这个美国人典型性格的特殊时代的结束。美国人可能开始变得不那么个人主义，不那么独立。


The frontier thesis was pretty much the accepted paradigm of the history of the American west until the 1970s. Then, for a variety of reasons, historians studying the American west began to question this thesis. And ultimately many of them ended up pretty much turning it on its head. 

直到20世纪70年代，“前沿”理论基本上是美国西部历史的公认典范。然后，出于各种原因，研究美国西部的历史学家开始质疑这一论点。最终，他们中的许多人几乎彻底推翻了之前的观点。



This is what fascinates me about this debate. The change that happened was not so much a matter of people finding new evidence that hadn't been available to Turner. It was more a question of people looking at the same evidence with a different eye, with different emphases. (3)

接下来是这场辩论让我觉得有意思的地方。所发生的变化并不是因为是人们找到了T所没有的新证据，而是因为人们用不同的眼光来看待同一证据。


There are two main areas of challenge to Turner that arose in what's been called the new western history. One was that Turner tended to overemphasize the importance of Americans of European descent on the western frontier.

新西部历史（派）对T提出了两个大的挑战。一是T倾向于过分强调欧洲后裔美国人在西部边境的重要性。


This was somewhat of an unconscious bias on his part. But you know, if you look to Turner's picture of the frontier, it was basically white men from Europe who set the tone and define the culture. (6) 

这在某种程度上是他无意识的偏见。但你知道，如果你看看T的边境图片，（这些照片）基本上是来自欧洲的白人决定了基调，定义了文化。


A new western historians took another look at the west and realized that it was actually a pretty culturally diverse place. You had, obviously, American Indians, Mexicans, Chinese, and others. So, the cultural uniformity of the frontier that turner saw, the new western historians look at it and see something very very different. 

一位新西部历史学家重新审视了西方，意识到它实际上是一个有着多元文化的地方。很明显，这里有美国印第安人、墨西哥人、中国人和其他人。因此，对于T所看到的西部开发前沿文化的一致性，新西部历史学家有完全不同的看法。


The other main area of challenge by the new western historians was that Turner really, in their view, underestimated the role of the federal government and large corporations in shaping the economic life of the frontier. (4) (5-A) 

新西部历史学家提出的另一个主要挑战是，在他们看来，T低估了联邦政府和大公司在塑造边境经济生活方面的作用。


I mean just as a for instance, the federal government to this day is the largest landowner west of the Mississippi river. And people's lives when they got to the frontier were really dictated by market forces beyond, pretty much beyond their control. You know, you grew wheat, you exported it to Europe, and whether you prosper or not depended at least partly on the price of wheat, which you had relatively little control over. 

举个例子，联邦政府至今是密西西比河以西最大的土地所有者。当人们到达边境时，他们的生活实际上是由市场力量支配的，远远超出了他们自己的控制。人们种植小麦，将其出口到欧洲，他们生活的好坏至少部分取决于小麦的价格，而他们对小麦的价格控制却没有什么控制权。


Ah, so these two main areas of challenge result in a very different picture of the western frontier. The new western historians see a culturally diverse place, a place where people weren't really in charge of their destiny. Even before the end of the frontier, people and their destinies were controlled by large impersonal forces, federal policy, changes in the prices of agricultural products, things like that. 

这两个主要的挑战领域导致了一个对西部边境截然不同的描绘。新西部历史学家看到了一个文化多样的地方，一个人们并不真正掌控自己命运的地方，即使是在“前沿”仍然存在的时候，人民的命运也是被巨大的非个人力量，即联邦政策、农产品价格的变化等等所控制的。


For Turner, the end of the availability of cheap land marks a crucial change in United States history. But for the new western historians, it's really more of the same continuation of the same kind of conflicts that had gone on in the days before the so called closing of the frontier. 

对T来说，失去可用的廉价土地标志着美国历史上的一个重要变化。但对于新西部历史学家来说，这只是“前沿”消失之前发生的同类冲突的延续而已。


So, again, as I say, it's so interesting because it's a case of a shift in perception rather than a shift in the underlying evidence that guided the initial perception. 

所以，正如我所说，这很有意思，因为这是一个观念转变的例子，而不是指导最初观念的潜在证据的转变。
