因为使玉米作为家养植物非常好的特点，使得它不适合野生生长了。

如果你看一下T的籽粒，看一下它们是如何固定在植物上的，你会发现一根独茎。


15) And the kernels are very loosely held to the stalk so that they are easily disturbed. (3-B)

T的籽粒非常松散地附着在茎上，所以它们很容易被弄乱散落。


16) Um, this is desirable for a wild plant, because the seeds need to be distributed.

对于野生植物来说这是很好的特征，因为种子需要被传播。

这些籽粒非常坚硬。

由于坚硬且非常松散，动物可以把它们捡起来并带走，它们可以吃很多，但它们不会把它们全部消化掉。

相比之下，如果你看一下家养化的玉米棒子，它的果核是柔软的。

它们排成多行，被一个外壳覆盖，将所有玉米粒紧紧地固定在一起。


21) This husk covered cob is great for people, cause we have a nice package that we can either eat or transport, doesn't fall apart easily. (4)

这种外壳覆盖的玉米棒子对人来说是很好的，因为有这个很好的“外包装”，我们吃或运输时它不容易散开。

玉米和T的花粉都是由风传播的。

T的花粉很轻，所以当风把它吹起来的时候，它真的会飘远，飘得非常远。


24) So one teosinte plant can pollinate another far away, but corn pollen is heavier, and it can only travel short distances in the wind.

因此，一株T可以为另一株远处的T授粉，但玉米花粉较重，只能在风中短距离传播。


25) In order to get a successful pollination(授粉), farmers know they need to plant at least three rows of corn. (5)

为了获得成功的授粉，农民知道他们需要至少种植三行玉米。


26) This gives enough density for the wind to carry enough pollen to the adjoining plants.

这个密度才能让风将足够的花粉带到相邻的植物上。


27) And of course, if the plant is not pollinated, you do not get a corn seed, so the corn will not grow unless proper pollination occurs.

当然，如果植物没有授粉，就不会有玉米种子，所以除非成功授粉，否则玉米就不会生长。


28) So it is highly unlikely that even if a corn seed was planted in the wild by itself, that it would ever encounter the pollen of another corn plant.

因此，即使玉米种子被单独种植在野外，它也极不可能遇到另一株玉米的花粉。


29) They don't self pollinate, so it is required that the pollen of another corn plant be used. (5)

它们不会自我授粉，所以需要使用另一株玉米的花粉。

另一个方面是玉米的产量很高，它生长得很快，而且你在每个果穗上都能得到很多的玉米粒。


31) We've isolated the genes that provide these desirable quantities of corn, we know, for instance, what gene makes the kernel soft and not hard.

我们已经分离出了提供这些理想数量的玉米的基因（其实就是高产），例如，我们知道是什么基因使玉米粒变得柔软而不硬。


32) We know what gene provides us with hundreds of kernels on one cob, rather than the five or six that you find in teosinte.

我们知道是什么基因使我们在一个棒子上有数百个玉米粒，而不像T只有五，六个。


33) In fact, if you were trying to match the production, the current corn production in the United States using teosinte, you would have to plant ten times the amount of farmland to grow enough teosinte. 

事实上，如果想通过种植T达到目前美国玉米种植的同等产量，你得种植十倍面积的农田。


34) Now keep in mind that these genes were selected and developed by people who did not have our technological skills.

大家别忘了，这些基因是由没有我们所拥有的技术能力的人选择和开发的。

试想一下，10000年前，仅仅通过观察和一些简单的工具，这些，这种奇妙的植物，这种作物就被开发出来了。

6.According to the professor, what could decompiculture be used for?


A.Studying termite behavior under experimental conditions

B.Protecting termite colonies from chemical waste

C.Manufacturing paper products that decompose fast

D.Cleaning up certain types of pollution


















答案：

C AC BD A C D





文本：


Listen to part of a lecture in an ecology class.


Professor: Termite, insect order, etc. are hard to think of them as more than just pests that want to eat your house. But I think you will be surprised at how interesting they are and how important they are to the ecosystem. Now, what do you already know about them?

教授：白蚁和一些其他的昆虫通常都被我们当做吃掉我们房子的害虫。但我想你会惊讶于它们是多么有意思以及它们对生态系统有多么重要。关于白蚁你们了解多少？


Male Student: They eat wood, lots of it.

男学生：他们吃很多的木头。（好的，大聪明。）


Professor: Right. To be more specific, they eat dead wood like dead trees in forests and plant litter. Their consumption of wood is like, you can think of this like recycling. Termites are recycling that litter back into soil. Their major source of nutrition comes from cellulose. The main chemical ingredient of wood and the cell walls of other plants, leaves, grasses etc. Now, cellulose is a complex sugar and most termites can't actually digest it.

教授：对。更具体地说，它们吃死木头，比如森林里的死树还有植物凋落物。他们对木材的消耗就像，你可以把这想象成回收利用。白蚁正在将垃圾回收回土壤中。它们的主要营养来源是纤维素。木材和其他植物、叶子、草等的细胞壁的主要化学成分。纤维素是一种复杂的糖，大多数白蚁实际上无法消化它。


Students: Uh?

学生：哦？


Professor: Because they don't have the right enzyme to metabolize it, to release its nutritional benefit. Now that would be a problem. But they have a special relationship with protozoa and bacteria that actually live in their digestive system.

教授：因为他们没有合适的酶来代谢它，释放它的营养。这本应是个问题。但它们与生活在体内消化系统中的原生动物和细菌有着特殊的关系。


Male Student: Inside?

男学生：体内？


Professor: Yes, it's these microscopic organisms that break down the cellulose for the termites. They'd starve without them. A good example of?

教授：是的，正是这些微生物为白蚁分解纤维素。没有他们，他们会挨饿的。这一个什么关系的例子？


Male Student: Symbiosis, two different kinds of organisms live together, so it's good for one or both of them. (2-A)

男学生：共生，两种不同的生物生活在一起，对其中一方或双方都有益的关系。


Professor: Good. What else is interesting? Termites are social. They live in colonies like ants or bees. Now about the colonies, each termite colony is founded by a male and female pair that mate and produce more termites. The termites in the colony come in several types called castes. There is the reproductive pair, the workers that gather food to feed the colony and soldiers that defend the colony against predators like ants. And they communicate by?

教授：很好。白蚁还有什么有意思的地方呢？白蚁是群居的。它们像蚂蚁或蜜蜂一样生活在群体中。关于白蚁群落，每个白蚁群落都是由一对雄性和雌性白蚁建立的，它们交配并产生更多的白蚁。蚁群中的白蚁有几种类型，称为阶层。有一对负责繁殖的白蚁（蚁王和蚁后），有收集食物喂养蚁群的工人，也有保护蚁群免受蚂蚁等掠食者袭击的兵蚁。那么，他们如何交换信息呢？


Female Student: Dancing? Like the dances bees do?

女生：跳舞？就像蜜蜂一样？


Professor: You are not too far off. Every colony has its own distinct smell, a scent that other termites within their colony recognize.(3-D) If a worker finds a new food source, it secretes a trail of scent that others can follow to locate the food and scent plays an important role in helping soldiers defend the colony against predators. If they detect an unfamiliar scent, let's say an ant is lurking around, the ant has a distinct smell, as soon as soldiers detect this ant scent, it's a warning. They shift into an aggressive mode ready to start defending their colony. Termites also communicate alarm through sound. If workers or soldiers want to sound the alarm, they'll bang their heads against the tunnels in their nest. Other termites perceive the vibrations through special sensory organs on their legs and come running to defend their colony. (3-B) So, they are pretty complex animals. I hope you are getting a sense of that.

教授：你说的八九不离十。每个蚁群都有自己独特的气味，这是蚁群中其他白蚁都能识别的气味。如果一只工蚁发现了新的食物来源，它会分泌出一种气味，其他人可以根据气味来定位食物，而气味在帮助士兵防御捕食者方面也起着重要作用。如果他们发现了一种不熟悉的气味，比如一只蚂蚁潜伏在周围，蚂蚁有一种独特的气味，一旦士兵发现这种蚂蚁的气味，这就是一个警告。他们转变成一种攻击性的模式，准备开始保卫他们的领地。白蚁也通过声音发出警报。如果工人或士兵想敲响警报，他们会用头撞蚁穴里的隧道。（真头铁）其他白蚁通过腿上的特殊感觉器官感知振动，并跑来保卫领地。所以白蚁是非常复杂的动物。（绝对不是吃木头的害虫那么简单）我希望你能意识到这一点。


Eventually a colony swarm... Sorry, got ahead of myself.

最终一个蚁群飞到。。。对不起，讲得有点超前了。（前面的内容还没讲完）


Only termites that can reproduce have wings. And that's only so they can fly to new location, find a mate and establish a new colony. So, eventually the special winged termites fly off in enormous numbers to form new colonies. They break off their wings after they land. In fact, that's how I knew I had an infestation in my house a few years ago. ④ Pretty amazing sight. Nothing I ever wanted to see in my house. Eww! There were so many wings on the floor and window sills. Eww! I called an exterminator to get rid of them but I kind of had mixed feelings about it.

只有能繁殖的白蚁才有翅膀。只有这样它们才能飞到新的地方，找到配偶，建立新的蚁群。因此，最终这些特殊的带翅膀白蚁会大量飞走，形成新的群体。他们着陆后会脱落翅膀。几年前当我在自己房子了看见这些翅膀的时候，我就知道房子里有白蚁了。 神奇的一幕，但是我可不想在自己家看到。呃啊！地板和窗台上全都是白蚁翅膀。我打电话给一个灭虫员想除掉他们，但我感情又很复杂。（大约是不太忍心杀死如此神奇的白蚁吧。）


You're kidding. 

It's true. See, the problem with destroying termite colonies is that they're really so crucial. Remember I said they recycle plant litter. In addition to creating new soil, termites themselves are a major source of food for other organisms such as salamanders, frogs, lizards and birds which are preyed upon by other organisms. They are part of an ecological cycle. So, it has an impact if too many termites are removed from the ecosystem. ⑤

您在开玩笑吧。（心疼白蚁你是认真的吗？）

真没有。破坏白蚁群落可能会导致问题，因为它们对环境真的很重要。记得我说过他们回收植物垃圾。除了创造新的土壤外，白蚁本身也是其他生物的主要食物来源，如蝾螈、青蛙、蜥蜴和鸟类等，（这一大堆不用记）这些生物又被其他生物捕食。它们是生态循环的一部分。因此，如果太多白蚁从生态系统中移除，就会产生影响。


Male Student: Sorry professor. But I’d be more concerned about they eating my house.

男学生：对不起，教授。但我还是更担心它们会吃掉我的房子。


Professor: As I was, which reminds me of an article I wanted to tell you about. Someone recently coined a term Decompiculture and let me write that down. Decompiculture is the idea that humans should cultivate organisms that can decompose our waste. The professor suggests in his article that we intentionally raise them, humans create a lot of waste that often gets dumped into landfills or water sources. It's really just pollution. Well, this waste can be fed to termites. Waste like?

教授：我当时也是一样。这让我想起了我想分享一篇文章。最近有人创造了一个术语叫D，让我把它写下来。D是指人类应该培养能够分解垃圾的生物。这位教授在他的文章中建议，我们应该饲养它们，因为人类制造了大量废物，这些废物常常被倾倒到垃圾填埋场或水源中，这些都是污染。这些垃圾可以喂白蚁。什么类型的垃圾呢？


Male Student: From saw mills or paper mills. So, termites could help us, huh.

男学生：来自锯木厂或造纸厂。所以，白蚁可以帮助我们。


Professor: Yes. When you think about, it is actually another kind of symbiotic relationship that humans would cultivate with termites. (2-C) / (6)

教授：是的。想想看，这实际上是人类与白蚁培养的另一种共生关系。
