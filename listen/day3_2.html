嗯，历史记录是不完整的，但我们已经能够确定一些趋势和一般定价准则。

通常价格取决于艺术作品的大小，大型作品的价格较高。

例如，风景画家开始使用较少的颜料颜色，以加快生产速度。

Listen to part of a lecture in a food science and technology class. 


One of the most popular beverages worldwide is coffee. You're probably not surprised to hear that. Here in the United States, for example, more than half of the adult population drinks at least one cup of coffee every day. For various reasons, some of those who drink coffee choose to drink decaffeinated coffee, either some of the time or all of the time. So the coffee industry had to face this sort of need or demand for decaffeinated coffee. 


咖啡是世界上最受欢迎的饮料之一。你大概不会对此感到惊讶。例如，在美国，一半以上的成年人每天至少喝一杯咖啡。出于各种原因，一些喝咖啡的人会选择喝不含咖啡因的咖啡。因此，咖啡行业不得不面对这种对无咖啡因咖啡的需求。


Taking caffeine out wasn't the problem. It was actually taking the caffeine out without changing the flavor of the coffee or without increasing the health risk to drinking the coffee. And, although been decaffeinated for probably just about a century, at this point, there is one relatively new method that's fairly commonly used these days to decaffeinate coffee. And that's supercritical fluid extraction. Supercritical fluid, they were, it's on the board, we can't understand the process of extracting the caffeine from the coffee if we don't get this concept down pat. ⑥ 


把咖啡因去掉不是问题。在不改变咖啡味道或不增加饮用咖啡的健康风险的情况下去掉咖啡因才是难点。而且，虽然去咖啡因的历史已经有大约一个世纪了，现在又有一种相对较新的方法被经常用于去除咖啡中的咖啡因。这就是SFE。SF，黑板上写了，如果我们不了解这个概念，我们就无法理解从咖啡中提取咖啡因的过程。



In order to do that, let's take a look at the phase diagram. This should be a review. The phase diagram, it shows the conditions of pressure and temperature, which a substance exists in 1 of 3 phases, a solid, a liquid and a gas. So we've got pressure on the vertical axis, temperature on the horizontal. A substance will change its phase if you start changing temperature or pressure. For example, if we choose sort of an arbitrary pressure right about here, and keep the pressure constant and increase the temperature, we start as a solid, but as you can see, just follow the line going to the right, as we increase the temperature, the solid changes to a liquid, And then changes to a gas. And that's what we would expect. We certainly know that happens with ice. It goes from a solid to a liquid and to a gas, if we keep the pressure constant.


为了解释清楚这个概念，让我们看看这个图表。这应该是复习了。这个图表显示了一种物质存在于固态、液态和气态分别对应的压力和温度。垂直轴是压力，水平轴是温度。如果你开始改变温度或压力，物质就会改变状态。例如，如果我们在这里选择一种任意的压力，保持压力不变并升高温度，起始状态为固态，当我们升高温度时，固体变成液体，然后变成气体。这是我们预期会发生的情况。我们很确定冰就同样的条件下就会这样发生变化。如果我们保持压强不变（并升高温度），它会从固体变成液体，再变成气体。



Now, let's do it the other way and keep the temperature constant. Let's take a couple of arbitrary temperatures and increase the pressure. (2) We start with gas. And we go up from the x axis and we see that gas will change either to a solid or to a liquid, depending on what temperature we are keeping constant. There is, however, a temperature at which if we exceed that temperature, regardless of how much we increase the pressure, this gas will not change into a liquid or a solid. (3) Let's call this critical temperature point TC. So there won't be a full phase change. There will be a change in its properties, however, it won't behave like a gas anymore. It will have the properties intermediate between the liquid and the gas. And we refer to it as a supercritical fluid at that point.


现在，让我们换一种（调节变量的）方法，保持温度不变。让我们任意取几个温度，然后增加压力。起始状态为气态。我们从x轴向上看，气体要么变成固体，要么变成液体，这取决于我们保持恒定的温度。然而，有一个温度，在这个温度下，如果我们超过这个温度，无论我们增加多少压力，这种气体都不会变成液体或固体。 我们称之为临界温度TC。所以（气体）不会有完全的状态改变。它的性质会发生变化，但是它的行为不再像气体了。它的性质介于液体和气体之间。在这一点上，我们称之为SF。



It's in the upper right hand corner here. So that means it only exists at high pressure and high temperature.(3) So that's what a supercritical fluid is. Now, what about these properties? 


它在这里的右上角。这意味着它只存在于高压和高温下。这就是超临界流体。那么，这些属性（又有什么意义）呢？


Listen carefully. A supercritical fluid diffuses like a gas and yet, it’s dense like a liquid. The significance of this is that a supercritical fluid can go through, can permeant a solid substance,(4-A) for example, a coffee bean because it diffuses like a gas and it can also dissolve other substances like caffeine, much like a liquid can because of its density. (4-B)

These particular properties or the values of these properties can be fine-tuned or adjusted by changing the pressure and the temperature. 

听好了。SF像气体一样扩散，但密度像液体。这一点的意义在于，超临界流体可以穿透、渗透固体物质，例如咖啡豆，因为它像气体一样扩散，它也可以像液体一样溶解咖啡因等其他物质，由于它（类似液体）的密度。这些特性都可以通过改变压力和温度来进行调整。


Now the way this is used in decaffeinating coffee is if we take the coffee beans and put them in a tank that can withstand extremely high pressures, and then we take supercritical carbon dioxide and pass it through the tank. So it's passing over the coffee beans. It actually permeates the coffee beans.(5-B) And, if we have the pressure and temperature just right, the caffeine will selectively dissolve into the supercritical carbon dioxide.(5-D) Together, the supercritical carbon dioxide and dissolved caffeine will pass out of that tank and into a second tank leaving decaffeinated coffee beans behind. Then we have a pressure relief valve on that second tank.(5-C) The pressure decreases and the supercritical carbon dioxide changes back into a gas, which then releases the caffeine in a crystallized form.(5-A)


（SF）在脱咖啡因咖啡中是这样使用的：如果我们把咖啡豆放在一个能承受极高压力的容器中，然后我们让超临界（S态）二氧化碳从中通过。二氧化碳会穿过咖啡豆，渗透到咖啡豆中。并且，如果我们的压力和温度刚好合适，咖啡因将选择性地溶解在超临界二氧化碳中，超临界二氧化碳和被溶解的咖啡因将从该容器中流出，进入第二个容器中，在第一个容器里留下脱咖啡因的咖啡豆。第二个容器上有一个减压阀，压力降低，超临界二氧化碳变回气体，然后以结晶形式释放咖啡因。
