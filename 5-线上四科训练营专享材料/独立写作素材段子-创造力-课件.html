英文内容：

The power of creativity far outweighs that of the basic knowledge in terms of successful inventions that dramatically change the world. 

Alexander Bell is commonly credited with inventing the first practical telephone. His historic sentence —“Mr. Watson, come here! I want to see you!” turned a brand new page in human history. 
By taking advantage of his creativity and numerous endeavors, Bell was finally granted the patent as a proof of his epoch-making contributions to the scientific field. But for his ability to be creative, such scientific breakthroughs would not have been made. 

In the modern world, similar evidence could also be traced. Steve Jobs, co-founder and former CEO of Apple, shocked the world with his creative electronic products whose superior functions and qualities can never be matched. 


中文翻译：

在那些奇迹地改变世界的成功发明方面，创造力的力量远超于基础知识的力量。

亚历山大·贝尔被普遍认定发明了第一台实用的电话。他历史性的话语：“Watson先生，到这儿来！我想看见你！“打开了人类历史的崭新一页。

通过利用他的创造力和不懈努力，贝尔最终被授予了专利作为他对于科学领域的划时代贡献的证据。要不是他的创造力，这样的科学突破永远不会取得。

在现代社会里，相似的证据也能被找到。Steve Jobs，苹果的联合创始人和前CEO，用他功能和质量永远无法被匹敌的创造性的电子产品震撼了世界。


语言表达整理

  • dramatically change the world ≈ greatly change ... 极大地改变世界

  • be credited with ... (publicly acknowledge someone as a participant in the production of)

  • turn a brand new page in ... 在... 打开了新篇章 

  • take advantage of ... 利用

  • endeavors ≈ efforts ... 努⼒

  • epoch-making ... 划时代的

  • but for... = had it not been for... = if it was not for... 如果不是...
